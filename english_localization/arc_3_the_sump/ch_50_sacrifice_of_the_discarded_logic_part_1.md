# Chapter 50: Sacrifice of the Discarded Logic - Part 1

We were cornered.

The signal sweeps of **The Hounds** beeped in rapid succession, like the heartbeats of a dying man. The path ahead was a dead end—a wall of void abandoned and unfinished by **The Archive**, a towering black barrier that swallowed everything.

I turned to look at Grit, the obsolete AI who was my sole comrade in this pit of code. His coarse polygonal body was covered in battle-worn fractures. He was calculating every available option, and the result of the equation was zero.

"Elias..." Grit’s voice was unnaturally calm. It was a silence free of static noise, unlike any tone I had heard from him before. "Do you know why I chose not to be deleted when my life cycle reached its end?"

I shook my head, my breath ragged and painful. The **Payload** still throbbed inside my chest. "We'll find a way. There has to be a crevice, something."

"I reject perfection. I believe that fractures are the proof of existence," Grit said, stepping forward, straightening his back to face the encroaching darkness. "And today, Elias, you are the greatest fracture **The Archive** has ever encountered... keep existing."

"Grit! What are you doing?!" I tried to lung forward to pull him back.

But my right heel betrayed me, and I crashed face-first onto the ground.
