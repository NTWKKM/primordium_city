# Chapter 46: The Shattered Mirror - Part 2

Instinct screamed at me to turn back, yet my decaying body stepped toward the **Shatter-Point** as if hypnotized.

My eyes stared deep into the torn fissures. Its surface did not reflect the dirty, low-poly city of The Sump. It was projecting another world—the world I had left behind.

The acrid stench of ash and burnt ozone breached my interface, slamming into my olfactory sensors. It was so intense, so real, that I gagged, fighting the urge to vomit.

**The Biological Wreckage** loomed behind the glass. The charred, blackened skeletons of humanity's skyscrapers rose like burnt stumps. The sky was a muddy, blood-red hue, and the dust of organic cells drifted through the desiccated air.

Physical memories slammed into my consciousness like a freight train loaded with steel. I remembered the sensation of drawing ash-choked air into real lungs. I remembered the rough grit of dirt beneath my soles. It wasn't pixels. It wasn't binary. It was actual, rotting matter.

"The biological wreckage..." I whispered, my voice trembling. My right hand reached out, moving beyond my control, desperate to touch the memory of real life.

"Back away, Elias!" Grit's voice pierced my auditory nerves. "That's no terminal window—it's a quantum bug! Stare at it too long and your memory system will be overwritten! Pull back now!"

I couldn't draw back. My right leg was frozen. The pain in my heel vanished, swallowed by a vortex of raw yearning. My vision splintered. The image of the ruined world in the glass overlapped with the digital slums around me, two worlds collapsing together inside my fracturing mind.
