# Chapter 46: The Shattered Mirror - Part 1

As the blood-red scanner waves of The Hounds faded, **The Silence Vacuum** once again enveloped The Sump. We had survived by a hair's breadth. Grit nodded, signaling for us to crawl out of the shelter.

The moment I stepped out, the whispers began their symphony.

It was no acoustic wave traveling through simulated air. It reverberated deep within the subroutine layers beneath my skull—a sourceless echo, nauseating, like a massive overdose of malware.

We were intruding deeper into a zone far more warped than before. Structural architecture twisted, laws of physics lay torn. I dragged my frame through the ruins of coarse geometric structures. Every time my right heel struck the ground, emptiness flared as phantom pain, wrenching my simulated nerves. I was a broken gear stubbornly forcing itself to spin, my left arm dangling—a numb lump of dead meat choked with **Bit Rot**.

"Do you hear it?" I groaned through gritted teeth, the taste of rust and degraded code thick in my mouth.

"My processing code isn't designed to read those garbage frequencies, Elias," Grit said, following behind, his voice jittering. "Even so, the data dimension ahead is collapsing. Some kind of logical gravity is clustering there."

We navigated through narrow alleys, the walls randomly shifting shapes due to memory faults. Even a passing touch sent tens of thousands of volts of static electricity shocking down my spine. Yet, the echo pulled me forward with a stronger gravity—an invisible hook caught in my throat, dragging me onward.

A lightless plaza opened up before us.

A colossal sheet of glass leaned against the ruins. It was not the soft, glowing save point of the Mid-Sectors. It was a **Shatter-Point**.

A mirror fractured so deeply that the cracks penetrated the fundamental logic of the dimension. These cracks did not break into shards; they tore open as rifts in the universe. A cold digital sweat seeped from my forehead, my breath caught.
