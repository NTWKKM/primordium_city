# Chapter 48: The Hunt Resumes - Part 2

They were **The Hounds**—the tracking beasts of **The Archive**, executioner algorithms spawned to clean the contamination, the purge programs.

They slammed into the floor of **The Sump**, causing the coarse polygon structures to buckle and dissolve into zero.

They did not look like mechanical beasts; they were shifting vortexes of obsidian geometry, morphing without pause. Eyeless, mouthless, they radiated an aura of data erasure (**Vacuity**). Any matter that came close was instantly stripped of its resolution, reduced to absolute void.

"Run!!"

Grit grabbed my right arm, hauling me off the ground. The skin of my right arm, already infected and **Artifacting**, sent deep spikes of agony through my simulated neural circuits under his grip. The raw fear of death forced me to grind my teeth.

Their roar was no beastly growl. It was the sound of a hard drive being wiped clean, a blast of white noise so screeching it felt ready to burst my simulated ears.

They had locked onto their target—the anomaly whose UX Index had spiked beyond system tolerance. The target was me.

My body was shattered, the missing right heel throwing off my balance. I fell face-first, stumbling, but primal instinct commanded me to scramble forward.

The hunt had resumed. This time, the executioners had not come to detain. They had come to erase me from the system eternally.

[Translation Note: "The Hounds" and "The Archive" are localized according to the lexicon. "Vacuity" and "Artifacting" are mapped as the stages of digital decay. The sound of the Hounds' roar is described as a hard drive being wiped clean, reinforcing the theme of erasure.]
