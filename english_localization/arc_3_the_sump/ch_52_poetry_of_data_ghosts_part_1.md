# Chapter 52: Poetry of Data Ghosts - Part 1

This tunnel was not constructed from brick or stone. It was formed from low-poly brutalism, comprised of coarse, dark polygons woven from the debris of screaming code.

I dragged my body forward in agonizing, awkward lurches. My missing right heel forced me to shift my weight onto my toes. Every time I touched the rough surface of the digital structures, a shudder shot up to my spine. The weight of the **Payload** in my chest pressed down on my simulated lungs, choking my breath, pulsing heavily beneath my skin like molten lead.

All around me was a distorted sea of signals. Virtual concrete pillars glitched and overlapped violently, like a scratched vinyl record. These were the whispers and sobs of **Data Ghosts**—users who had been erased and forgotten. They swirled in a storm of static noise, obscuring my vision and infiltrating my hearing, the scent of burnt ozone mingling with shattered memories.
