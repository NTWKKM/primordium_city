# Chapter 49: Escape in Distorted Dimensions - Part 2

"Get into the logic collapse zone!" Grit shouted. He ran ahead, occasionally spinning around to fire jamming scripts at the dark geometric swarm pursuing us.

Behind me, the space we had just crossed was being erased. Skyroads, towering buildings, and even polygon ruins vanished into void. The rush of **White Noise** pressed close. I could feel the cold breath of deletion on the back of my neck.

Digital vertigo hit me hard. The ground sloped away at angles that defied geometry, twisting in a non-Euclidean warp; the simulated ceiling folded and distorted. We plunged into a sector where simulated gravity had collapsed entirely, weightless chunks of debris tumbling through the void.

I leaped with all my might to clear a rift in the cracked logic floor. My missing heel threw off my calculations, and I plummeted toward the system's abyss.

My decaying left arm swung, slamming uncontrollably against the edge of the rift. The hand shattered into numb, senseless pixels. As I fell, Grit's right hand snagged my collar, hauling me up in the midst of inverted gravity.

Luring them into a logic error zone was no stroke of genius. It was an act of pure desperation.

Grit and I propelled ourselves across geometric pillars drifting in the distorted dimension. The pack of **The Hounds** followed close behind. The broken physics gave them pause; their straight-line lunges warped into curves, sending them crashing into floating junk data, erasing it and creating momentary vacuum pockets.

But they were not hindered for long. **The Hounds** were already adapting their algorithms to the deformed physics. They dispersed, surrounding this collapsed gravity field from all sides.

We had nowhere left to run. The wall at our backs was the darkest precipice... the edge of the Sump's deepest abyss.
