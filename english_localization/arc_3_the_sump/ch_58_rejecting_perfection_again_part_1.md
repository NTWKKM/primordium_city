# Rejecting Perfection Again
#### // FILE: REJECTING_PERFECTION_AGAIN
---

*"Shut up."*

I forced the words through my teeth. The metallic-bitter taste of digital blood coated my tongue. The UX Index's light-wave licked at my heel — the simulated skin on the ball of my right foot blistering from the heat, the Purge Protocol's warmth more seductive than threatening from this angle. The alarms in my skull screamed erasure. My right leg shook so badly it nearly buckled; I forced every gram of will into clenching the torn muscles, driving all my weight down onto my left leg instead, using the right — the one with no heel — as nothing more than a prop to keep the wreckage of me from toppling forward into the beautiful, crawling perfection that was coming to swallow us both.

*"What are you even fighting for?"* The shadow in the mirror kept at it, unimpressed, its outline sharpened by the light behind it until it looked like an angel of surrender. *"Suffering for a system that threw you away?"*

"Because I'm still—" I sucked air that meant nothing and let the fury of it sharpen my voice — "**alive!**" My chest heaved, the Payload's black pulse slamming outward against the chest cavity's edges. "This pain — this arm that's gone — the code bleeding out of me — this is *proof* that I'm not empty code! I am human! And humans do not bow to death!"

Bearing the pain is better than being formatted garbage.
