# Sea of Shells
#### // FILE: SEA_OF_SHELLS
---

I kept my profile narrow, carefully weaving through the tight gaps between the bodies of The Hollows. Grit led the way, his eye flashing in rapid intervals as he mapped a path through the frozen thicket of dead souls.

"If you touch them," Grit explained, sidestepping a Hollow that was repeatedly beating its chest, "they will drain your resolution to patch the leaks in their own void."

I limped after him, dragging my phantom-pained right leg and keeping my numb left arm pinned tight against my side. The ground beneath us stuttered as the distorted gravity shifted. I lost my balance and stumbled, my left shoulder brushing the arm of a Hollow standing next to me.

*Szzzt!*

A sensation like having my soul ripped out flared through my body. The pixels on my shoulder blurred, dissolving into a spray of light particles that were instantly sucked into the Hollow’s form. Grit whipped around, grabbed me by the collar, and violently yanked me away.

"I told you to be careful!" Grit scolded, the light of his LED eye reflecting a deep, heavy melancholy. "They aren't trying to hurt you. They're just starving for a resolution they can never retrieve."

Compassion from a machine made of scrap pierced through me. It left a lump of raw grief in my throat, a dry choke that no simulated tear could ever flush out.
