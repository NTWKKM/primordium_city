# Crossing the Abyss
#### // FILE: CROSSING_THE_ABYSS
---

I was breathing in shallow, ragged pulls. Black data-blood dripped from the socket where my left arm no longer existed and from the chest cavity yawning open in my torso. I turned to look behind me one last time.

The UX Index's Purge Protocol had consumed The Sump almost entirely. The low, brutalist structures of the data slums were gone — replaced by the expanding void of clinical white. Where there had been ruin and filth and the architecture of desperation, there was nothing. Blank. Null.

In what remained of the wreckage still dissolving at the edges, there were only the echoes of Data Ghosts and the faint, fading trace of rust on the air. This was the place that had taken Grit from me. The place that had taken my arm, had taken the heel from my right foot, had opened the wound in my chest and filled it with something the system could not name. I hated every polygon of it with everything I had left. And yet — this filth, this darkness, this brutal geometry of suffering — this was what had *shaped* me into a key. What had broken me into something capable of opening the lock.

I carved a promise into the deepest register of my memory, below any layer the Purge could reach:

*Your sacrifice wasn't wasted... Grit.*

I fixed the image of the darkness being erased — memorialized the scar of its loss — and refused to let deletion take the people it had held.
