# Weapon of Decay
#### // FILE: WEAPON_OF_DECAY
---

I breathed a breath that didn't exist. I wrung every last gram of grief and loneliness out of myself, channeled the whole uncompressed mass of it directly into the fracture.

The petabyte-scale bandwidth surge sent a Backlash tearing through me like a quantum storm. My left arm — eaten hollow by the Bit Rot virus — could no longer bear the processing load it had been pushed past every threshold to sustain. The polygon structure gave a sound like glass being ground to powder.

In a single instant, starting from the fingertips and spreading upward past the elbow, my arm disintegrated into fragments of pure black pixel. Data-ash scattered and was swallowed by the silence. The pain drove through every firewall I had. I crumpled onto the invisible floor, and the fracture ahead of me widened slowly, slowly — bathing my face in the cold light bleeding up from The Core.

[Translation Note: The arm's final dissolution is rendered as a visceral geometric collapse — polygon-grinding glass — rather than a clean amputation. The "breath that didn't exist" preserves Elias's ironic awareness that he is not biological, yet performs biological desperation rituals. "Petabyte-scale Backlash" kept from source as a technical horror term. This is the arm's last moment; from Ch 56 onward, no left arm exists.]
