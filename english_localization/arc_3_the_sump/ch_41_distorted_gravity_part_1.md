# Distorted Gravity
#### // FILE: DISTORTED_GRAVITY
---

The moment I crossed into the darkness, the ground beneath my feet vanished. There was no wind to resist this fall, no physical drag of gravity like in the biological wreckage. It was a clean collapse of the Z-axis. My body was sucked down into the architectural abyss, before crashing onto a geometric floor that had never known the grace of beveled edges.

*Crack—*

The crack of breaking code echoed inside my simulated skull. The entire world stuttered, caught in a freeze. Waves of green-purple rot—the sickening hues of digital decay—splattered across my vision, alternating with the absolute black of void. The scene before me rendered in jerky, halting fits. This was **The Sump**, the data slums... a scrap heap abandoned by the divine algorithm.

Surrounding me was the starkness of low-poly brutalism. Street curbs rose with edges sharp as glass blades. Pixel garbage lay compressed, piled high into towering walls. The only illumination came from the dull, sickly sparks of short-circuiting data streams.

I forced myself up. The world tilted violently. A sudden rush of digital vertigo slammed into my auditory nerves. The laws of physics had been torn apart; gravity yanked me downward, then violently flung me sideways. I scrambled, clawing my fingers into the rough, jagged ground. The abrasions on my skin flickered as lines of red, warning-state code.

As I shifted my weight onto my right ankle, pain flared through my artificial nerves. It was phantom pain—my missing heel replaced by a sharp, screaming agony. Error signals screeched like nails driven into a non-existent wound, reminding me that I was still very much alive in this digital purgatory.
