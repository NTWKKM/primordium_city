# Chapter 49: Escape in Distorted Dimensions - Part 1

We lunged into a crevice of collapsed geometry to buy ourselves time.

The screeching signal sweep of **The Hounds** pressed hard against our backs, blood-red lasers sweeping furiously across the low-poly debris overhead.

I leaned my shivering back against a **Data Pillar**. Grit crouched beside me, clutching a fistful of jamming scripts, his coarse polygonal body covered in cracks.

Turning my face away from the scanner beams, my eyes struck a glossy surface embedded in the concrete wall. It was a **Shatter-Point**—a fractured mirror of reality, a rift in the quantum dimension.

I expected to see the reflection of my ruined avatar, the rotting stump of my left arm, or eyes filled with terror. What appeared instead was a far deeper aberration.

The image in the mirror was not me. It was **The Biological Wreckage**—a world of ash and decaying flesh. I saw a leaden ash sky, the ruins of the physical world, and a withered physical body hooked to tubes inside a capsule. The putrid truth hidden beneath the digital illusion. I stared at it, nausea rising to choke my throat, as the **Payload** in my chest throbbed violently in response to the horrifying sight.

The red beam swept down, splashing across my toes. Grit yanked my right arm.

"Run!"

We lunged forward. Every time I bore weight on my right foot, the deformity jolted straight to the core of my soul. I dragged myself through the ruined debris of The Sump in a mangled rhythm—limp, drag, crash—my left stump, heavy with **Bit Rot**, swinging uselessly like a torn rag doll.
