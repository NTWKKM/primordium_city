# Awakening at Point Zero
#### // FILE: AWAKENING_AT_POINT_ZERO
---

My consciousness drifted on the edge of the final code. Geometric lines of light from the void at Point Zero flared brighter and brighter amidst the slowly falling digital ashes. I watched the simulated residues of my body—the weight of my left arm, which had become a mere zero-bit void, and the pressure on my missing right heel—shedding away line by line, data block by data block.

Those lines of light intensified, pressing against the limits of processing power. The architecture of logic and reason was pushed to overload. The landscape, once composed of light and shadow, fractured into a haze of stark white pixels. It was not the pristine white of blank paper, but a white born from the fusion of every color, every frequency, and every emotion packed so densely that sensors could no longer differentiate between them. The light exploded, consuming everything around it, consuming me.

No body. No boundaries. No left or right, no past or future. Only a "now" expanding boundlessly.

I sank into a fluid consciousness, a state where all data flowed as one. I felt the current of trillions of bits and bytes dancing in a quantum state—potentializing everything and representing nothing all at once. The fear that had once crushed my soul dissolved, replaced by a weightless, dizzying drift.

The unrendered sun glowed dimly behind the white expanse, offering a warmth that arose not from calculated temperature, but from acceptance. This was Point Zero.

I felt a full emptiness, a perfect deficiency. The pulse of what equated to a "heart" in this world did not beat. Instead, it vibrated as a low-frequency wave, sinking deep into the core of nonexistence. I was the empty space that allowed all things to occur. I was the transition between destruction and genesis.

Within this infinite whiteness, I discovered that all the suffering I had carried throughout the fifth arc—the pain of digital sepsis, loss, and the struggle to preserve the resolution of my identity—was, in truth, merely an illusion we constructed to convince ourselves of our own importance. When we accept our deletion, when we allow our resolution to downscale to zero, we finally find the imprints hidden behind the veil of code.

True freedom is not having power over all things, but having nothing left to lose.

I drifted within this Digital Nirvana, savoring the taste of selflessness. It was beautiful, breathtakingly so. The residual human trace within me wept with joy—non-existent tears streaming down non-existent cheeks, a layered and contradictory feeling, yet strangely harmonious. I wished to freeze time right here, letting this current of consciousness soothe my soul for eternity.

A deep instinct in my database whispered that Point Zero was not the destination... it was the starting point of reconstruction. The world still needed an architect who understood cessation, someone to guide the emerging creations so they would not lose themselves to counterfeit perfection once again. I had to prepare for the grand expansion of a new self—one born not of flawlessness, but of the cracks of the most beautiful imperfections.
