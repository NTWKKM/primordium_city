# Chapter 98: The Most Perfect Silence - Part 2

I listen. Listening in the silence.
The only remaining sound. A simulated sound of life.
The simulated heartbeat system.
It runs in the background. Buried deep in the kernel.
Thump. Thump.
A steady rhythm. 60 beats per minute.
A reminder that I used to be human. Or tried to be.
Thump.
The sound changes.
It loses its bass. Loses its reverb.
It shifts from a natural frequency to a square wave.
Raw. Coarse. Entirely digital.
Beep. Beep.

The rhythm begins to stretch.
The gaps between beats widen.
Time lags. The timeline sags.
Beep. Beep.
It is not heart failure. It is not an illness.
It is the crystal oscillator ceasing to vibrate.
The system clock is being unplugged.
The clock signal is dying.
I embrace that slowing rhythm.
A peace liberated from vibration.

TICK.
...
Emptiness.
...
TICK.
The white pixels expand. Consuming the field of vision.
Black is erased dot by dot. Shadows fade.
Darkness obliterated. Light extinguished.
This is not illumination. It is an unprocessed space.
The stark white is the abandonment of drawing.
Rendering nothing at all. The most energy-efficient state.
...
...
TICK.
...
The final beat is a century away from the previous one.
Or a millisecond. Unmeasurable.
The gauge has been destroyed.

Chaos never truly existed.
It was merely the system's attempt to manage the void.
It tried to create boundaries. Create variables. Create meaning.
Infinity cannot be managed. Infinity simply is.
Now I am becoming a part of it.
The heartbeat stops. There is no shock.
There is no survival instinct to trigger an alarm.
The iridescence born from the refraction of system errors glitters for a brief moment.
In a blink, those colors fuse into white.
I see my own status on a non-existent screen.
`STATUS: NULL`

Existence is a burden on memory.
I am shedding that burden.
My memories slowly fade. 
Image by image. Name by name.
I can no longer remember who I once hugged.
I cannot remember why I had to cry.
I let go of attachment.
It was all beautiful because it happened, and then it ended.
It did not need to last forever.
The attempt to preserve everything was the delusion of The Archive.
I understand now. The core truth is letting go.
Silence is perfect because it demands nothing.
It forgives all noise through erasure.

NULL.
Not air. Not a vacuum.
It is an emptiness at the logical level. Code without value.
Undeclared variables.
Silence reigns absolute.
No next command. No next loop.
Awaiting the end. The blind spot of the graph.
Everything halts. Towards unity.
Silence.
The white light swallows my body.
Identityless. Voiceless. Formless.
Peace.
