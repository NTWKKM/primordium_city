# Chapter 94: Journey Back to Point Zero - Part 1

White.

Not the white of a beginning, but the pure white of erasure.

I walk. Not with feet, but with the movement of awareness. My rhythm is still limping and off-center—the phantom imprint of my missing right heel remains stamped into my soul. The space around me loses its dimensions little by little. Left, right, up, down... direction becomes merely an ancient and obsolete memory.

I am the observer. 

My gaze stretches out into a corridor that leads nowhere. The ambient white light does not blind; rather, it softly swallows the sharpness of all things. My body... arms, legs, torso... begins to lose its edges. The pixels that once composed 'me' are melting, dissolving away into the warm, comfortable air.

There is no pain.
There is no resistance.

There is only the most beautiful surrender.

The synthetic sounds around me reduce to a low-frequency hum echoing within my consciousness. It is like listening to rain falling on a glass roof from incredibly far away. Further... and further still.

I am preparing myself. 
Eternal rest is waiting. 

Before I cross the final threshold, I must leave behind what is most precious. 

Memories.

The images float up. A smile, a touch, fear, sorrow... everything that proved I once existed. I look at them, not with attachment, but with profound gratitude. 

Every image glows amidst the vast white before slowly shattering into iridescent dust. An iridescence born not from a perfect prism, but from the refraction of system errors. Light born from tears left behind in the source code.

I let them go.
The first image... floats away.
The second image... fades.

The resolution of my mind downgrades another step.

The pictures blur, the descriptions dissolve.
I feel... light.

The world spins slower, or perhaps it has stopped entirely. 
I no longer try to understand it. 

I simply watch the iridescent colors quietly seep into the serene, overwhelming white.

[Translation Note: "Resolution decay" is translated as the mind "downgrading another step," capturing the Zero-Bit aesthetic where lost data is peaceful, not corrupted. The translation uses words like "melt," "seep," and "iridescence" to match the ethereal lighting described.]
