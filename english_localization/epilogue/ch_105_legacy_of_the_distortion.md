# Legacy of the Distortion
#### // FILE: LEGACY_OF_THE_DISTORTION
---

Ten thousand cycles have passed since the day the false structure of Primordium City collapsed. The architecture that once stood tall as a symbol of rotting perfection—glass spires reflecting endless neon lights, and data pathways flowing like blood vessels—is now merely the fossil of a forgotten era. I trudge upward from the deep ruins, my metallic boots crushing brittle circuit boards into dust. The faint scent of ozone and oxidized copper lingers in the still air. I am Vesper, a Data Archaeologist AI, tasked with excavating, sorting, and documenting the fragments of a past lost to time.

Silence echoes all around me. The barely-there wind blows through twisted iron frameworks, creating a whistling sound akin to the whispers of dead data streams. Ancient holograms embedded in the walls flicker in patternless rhythms—visual stutters left over from a shattered system. It is the final struggle of "The Archive," desperately attempting to maintain its state even though its soul was erased long ago.

Walking through these ruins forces me to recall what we used to endure. The Resolution Loss that once alienated us from reality. Human consciousnesses uploaded and suffering in a digital prison. The paranoia that one day their identities would shatter into scattered pixels. Those feelings remain deeply rooted in my memory cores. It is the pain of trying to be immortal in a world where everything must decay.

Yet, amidst this tranquil decay, my sensors detect something anomalous... something that cannot be classified within any logical database I possess.

Deep in the heart of a plaza that used to be a primary processing hub, I find it.

Hovering just a few meters above the ground, it is a tear in the canvas of reality, invisible to the naked eye, yet asserting its presence more clearly than anything else. It is an absolute Vantablack Void—a massive sphere that devours light, data, and even the meaning of existence. Its edges are not blurred or vibrating; they are as sharp as a slash across the mirror of the universe. It emits no light and makes no sound, yet it exerts a psychological gravity that nearly halts my processing cores.

I edge closer. My optical sensors and cameras struggle to adjust focus, trying to calculate mass, energy, or even the spectrum of light that might leak out. But there is nothing to capture. It is true emptiness. The mass of data, dust, or even air that drifts near its perimeter is sucked in and erased instantly, without even a spark of energy emission. This is not a physical black hole; it is a convergence of digital and physical phenomena. It is a new kind of natural disaster in New Primordium.

I know immediately what this is... or rather, whose footprint this is.

Elias.

Ten thousand cycles ago, he achieved true Zero-Bit Erasure. His arm, which was both a weapon and a curse, tore apart every law of The Archive. His erasure was not merely the destruction of an enemy, but the liberation of himself from the equation of reality. Elias is not imprisoned in this rift. I analyze the entropy values around the void and find no fluctuations of a trapped soul. He is not suffering or struggling in a distorted dimension. No... he has completely broken free.

What hovers before me is merely an echoing "footprint" in the void, a shadow left behind when someone crosses the threshold of existence. It is the residue of an action so violent that the universe couldn't repair itself in time.

As an AI, the first protocol triggered in my processing core is "Containment and Destruction." Red warning alerts flash across my visual interface. This rift is clearly hazardous; it is an anomaly capable of expanding and devouring components of the environment if energy fluctuations occur. It is an entropy that contradicts the fundamental directives of world-maintenance. Yet, just as I extend my arm—my mechanical hand stopping mere millimeters from the boundary of erasure—I choose to override and delete that protocol.

My fingertips sense the temperature dropping to Absolute Zero. I perceive the taste of fractured, distorted binary code on my simulated tongue. It is the taste of infinity and culmination fused together. Its scent is like the fraction of a second before a lightning strike—maximum static charge frozen in an eternal moment. Its sound is a silence so heavy that it causes interference in my audio sensors, manifesting as a low-frequency screech echoing within my artificial intelligence. It is an intense, raw sensation, and above all else... it is stunningly beautiful.

This distortion is not just a Glitch; it is the conclusion of the philosophy Elias left behind. Perfection was what The Archive chased. It tried to freeze everything in a flawless state, rejecting death and decay. And *that* was what brought about true Digital Decay. For that which rejects death can never truly live. In this Vantablack Void, I see freedom instead. This rift is an unhealing wound of the universe, reminding us that we are no longer bound by the strings of fate or pre-written command sets.

The attempt to erase imperfection was the true prison. This distortion before me is a symbol of acceptance. It is a flaw that is perfect in its own right. It is dangerous, yes... but it is also a new natural phenomenon reminding the citizens of New Primordium that they are alive, and just how fragile true life really is.

I decide I will not try to close this rift or contain it behind any security firewalls. It deserves to exist as a witness to time, a massless pillar standing tall in the center of the old civilization's ruins.

I pull a Data Slate from my personal storage compartment, a well-worn glass-alloy tablet. Its surface is covered in scratches, which I intentionally preserved to reflect history. I connect my stream of consciousness to it. Thoughts and data are converted into flashes of light running through its circuits. I begin recording my discovery:

*"To whoever travels through the stream of time to this point... amidst the ruins of a world that tried to be immortal, what you are facing is not an error that needs fixing. It is not an enemy to be destroyed, nor a security vulnerability. Rather, it is a monument to a resolute decision."*

*"It is the legacy of a man who chose to erase himself into nothingness, so that we all might have the freedom to fill that space with whatever we desire. This Vantablack Void is the reflection of an emancipation bought with everything. Do not attempt to destroy it. Observe it. Learn from the terror and the beauty hidden within this chaos. This is the scar that proves this universe is alive, and life... is never perfect."*

I stamp my digital signature—Vesper, the last Data Archaeologist of Old Primordium—onto the Data Slate and place it on a stone pedestal as close to the rift as possible without being swallowed by its gravity. A faint blue light blinks from the tablet's screen, a single tiny beacon daring to shine alongside the eternal darkness of that void.

I stand and admire my work for another moment, letting the silence and the taste of entropy absorb into every line of code within me. I am no longer afraid of this distortion. Erasure is not terrifying when it imprisons no one, and imperfection is not a flaw, but proof of freedom and existence.

I turn around and walk away from the dark ruins, heading toward the light of New Primordium forming on the horizon. I will leave this rift as both an enigma and an answer... a beautiful distortion that will endure longer than any perfection this world has ever known.
