# The Null Coordinate
#### // FILE: THE_NULL_COORDINATE
---

How long had I been falling? I had no way to know.

The line between time and space had dissolved the moment I plunged through the Clipping Error. Code folded and tore around me during the descent. My avatar degraded with each passing fragment of a second — the form once assembled from flawless 32K algorithms peeling away, layer by layer, until only a shivering low-poly skeleton remained.

The Archive's security systems could not reach here. This was not an unrendered sector waiting to be populated. It was the space between the lines of code — the physical substrate that the system could not sweep without destroying its own foundation.

That was my pass.

When the wireframe stub at my right heel finally found a surface, the contact was nothing like I expected. Not the rigid compression of a circuit board. Not the smooth give of fiber-optic flooring. The ground beneath me yielded — slightly, elastically — like pressing down into a sediment of tissue. My low-res avatar stuttered, image tearing and recovering before stabilizing.

The atmosphere around me had changed completely. The darkness here wasn't the digital void of switched-off pixels. It was Vantablack — massive, dense, load-bearing, pressing down on my shoulders as though darkness had mass. It absorbed even the infrared spectrum of data-center heat.

I drew a breath that didn't exist. And I smelled something.

The familiar scorch of burning circuitry faded. In its place: the concentrated blood-tang of something old and sealed, iron rust, the fetid sweetness of meat left in a broken cold storage unit.

My audio receptors began to function. First the deep, industrial groan of cooling fans. The deeper I moved, the more the sound warped — stretched, slowed. The rhythm of the motor-spin converted into a pumping rhythm. Something like lungs breathing through fluid. Wet inhalation — wet exhalation — moist, dripping, labored.

I raised my damaged arm and pressed it against the corridor wall to brace myself. I expected metal alloy — cold, smooth. What my degraded haptic sensors delivered back made my processing core lurch.

The wall was warm. Thirty-seven degrees Celsius. Porous. Damp with beaded moisture — skin texture. Human skin flushed with a fever's sweat.

A dim light pulsed ahead — seeping through a gap in the wall, illuminating in stuttering glimpses what I was walking through.

The servers here were not built from silicon wafer or quantum cores. They were metal frames wrapped in thin biological membrane. Dark veins coiled around cable bundles. Pumping. Moving something dark red through the system.

The Trauma Shard embedded deep in the data-core of my chest began to tremble. It screamed at a frequency that cut down to the bone — not resonating with any code, not with any digital signal. It was resonating with *pain* — the raw, physical torment that saturated the very air of this place.

Hardware. Interface. Layer. The territory where software was forced to connect to the truth.

I dragged my limping, broken body down the tissue corridor. Each footfall echoed with a wet, flat smack. The truth I had come here to find was within reach. Every instinct I had left screamed to turn back — before everything I had believed came apart so completely there would be nothing left to reconstruct.
