# Escaping the Inner Ring
#### // FILE: ESCAPING_THE_INNER_RING
---

03:00. Three minutes.

I dragged my near-shattered form to the front of the Transit Gate, the sole exit that would plunge me into the decay below.

The footless shadow of the Sweepers pursued close behind. The only confirmation of their presence was the high-frequency vibration of space, warped by objects being hastily rendered as they surged across zones. They were gaining on me.

Digital Sepsis made my body feel heavily weighed down by garbage data. I could barely move my legs. Every shift of my weight was a grueling struggle against a frame rate that had collapsed below ten frames per second. My vision began to blur, sliding into stuttering, disjointed motions.

They drew so close that I could feel the ambient temperature plunge, a draft from the system diverting raw processing power to compute their pursuit.

I raised my right hand, touching this sector's master control panel. I summoned my 99% UX Index privilege one last time—not to pave a path, but to destroy.

I ordered a radical, absolute downgrade of the physics engine in the sector behind me. The laws of gravity and aerodynamics were instantly overwritten. The air mass compressed, turning into a turbulent, dense geometric field. The Sweepers, hurtling forward at high speeds, plowed into the zone and lost their anti-gravity stabilization. Their levitation code collided with the warped physics, dragging their footless forms crashing to the ground in a cacophony of failing computational math.

00:00. Time expired.

I threw myself into the shaft of the Transit Lift.

The instant the lift doors slammed shut and plummeted into the dark, my access rights to the Inner Ring were severed. My high-resolution quota was stripped completely.

The blinding 32K world was skinned away. The pure light was replaced by bruised purples and a filthy, dim sodium orange. The entire world was rapidly downsampled. The graphics around me fractured into macroblocking, the resolution collapsing to a mere 720p. The ambient audio—even the sound of my own gasping breath—was bit-crushed and muffled, sounding like static from a poorly tuned shortwave radio.

Then the Transit Gate's compression matrix kicked in, initiating the standard data compression sequence required for inter-zone travel to conserve bandwidth.

Yet it could not compress my twisted, sepsis-infected left arm.

"Aaargh!"

The agony pierced straight to my core processor, transcending physical pain. It was the sensation of the digital matter in my left arm being crushed, shredded, and rearranged by an algorithm desperate to squeeze corrupted data into a restricted bandwidth. The smell of scorched ozone billowed, accompanied by the taste of rust and copper pouring forth like blood.

I was falling, falling down the shaft of decay, leaving the counterfeit perfection behind in exchange for a shattered life in the shadows below.
