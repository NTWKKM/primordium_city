The scream compressed into Glitch Symbols — tearing out of Vesper, The Unpatched — before stuttering into fractured speech riddled with data voids.

"Perfection... ▒▒▒... is a cage with a painted window... decay... is evolution."

Vesper wasn't recoiling from the High-Res Aura. His hollow eyes were watching the waves of Bit Rot radiating from my chest with something approaching rapture — a disciple who'd found the god of ruin he'd been waiting for.

"You wear the filth convincingly... but only on the surface..." His voice shook, threaded through with static. "A high-priority target hiding here... [NULL_POINTER]... it's a Beacon. It will draw attention from above. The Great Purge will sweep every one of us. Why does a pristine fragment bring death to the fracture?"

My processing core hammered like fresh meat. I had to lie.

"I'm not here to hide." I forced my voice into a ragged, eroded texture — mimicking the tremor of something near collapse. "What I'm carrying from the biological realm — what's resonating inside me right now — it isn't a cure."

I pressed a hand against my chest. The epicenter of the Bit Rot radiation.

"It's the most perfect weapon ever extracted. A biological catastrophe specifically designed to eat the Inner Ring from the inside. I'm going to bring them the greatest decay that has ever existed. Their real evolution."

Vesper processed my words. The thinking in that fractured form moved fast — flickers of light and short-circuit sounds crackling through.

"Words... [SYSTEM_FAULT]... are cheap data." The leader's challenge emerged through a collapsing beep-tone. "If you're a true believer — prove it. Sever the umbilical of your perfection."

I understood immediately what he meant.

At the nape of my neck, a communications and tracking node was embedded — an antenna pulsing its signal upward to The Archive. As long as I kept it, the Great Purge would arrive sooner or later.

No time to hesitate. I raised my right hand, tore through the rotting Sepsis shroud wrapped around the arm, letting the outer layer of digital pus peel back and fall, revealing the pristine 32K code-structure concealed beneath. I ordered my undamaged fingers to reshape into data-claws and drove them through the digital skin at the back of my own neck.

*Aaagghh!*

Pain detonated without restraint. This wasn't file deletion. It was tearing a living nervous system from its housing. My clean code screamed as I extracted the tracking node — a blue, pulsing piece of myself, beating in my fist. Digital gold blood welled from the wound, spilling across the obsidian mirror shards below, dissolving into the Bit Rot reaction.

I crushed the node to fragments in my hand. The last filament of connection to The Archive fell into the dark.

But as long as I blazed with a 32K aura, I remained a visible target. Destroying the node had merely cut the telephone line. I was still a lit beacon from any distance. The pain scorched my vision white. What was worse than the wound itself was knowing: to truly hide, I had to extinguish my own light. I had to let the shock of the node extraction cascade through the whole system and take it down.

My conviction had become contaminated. Staring at the waves of decay radiating from my own body, I began to ask the question I'd been suppressing: what if what I was carrying was not a cure — but the end of this world?

I let the pain bury conscious thought. Vision blackened. My body fell, severed from connection, dropping into the depths the system refused to render — into the perfect dark of the Dead Zone.
