# The Deletion Sweep
#### // FILE: THE_DELETION_SWEEP
---

The connection was fraying. The Patch injected into my left arm to conceal my metadata was dissolving rapidly — the Slag-Tiers' computational heat eating through it like acid. If the junk code burned away entirely, The Archive would read my 99% UX Index in the clear, and the advancing wall of white light would lock onto me directly.

I hauled myself into the remains of a cooling tower — now nothing but a Wireframe skeleton of geometric lines blinking in and out, the structural mesh revealing an emergency data-drainage conduit sealed behind an ancient, corroded control panel.

No time for elegant intrusion. My left arm had no remaining strength for brute force. I pressed the junk-code-riddled forearm against the control panel and forced a short-circuit — unleashing blue sparks and a pulse of Sepsis directly into the door's architecture, letting the corruption serve as a ghost key.

*Crack!* The conduit cover blew open. A dark shaft gaped before me, pulling hot air upward in a steady draft.

The white light flooded in from behind. So bright it erased my shadow.

Then the Data Key detonated inside my skull — triggered by the system's scan wave. In that split second I was not in the Slag-Tiers. I saw myself in a dark hall lined with glass cylinders. Inside them: red organs, wet and pulsing. Cables webbed between them like veins pulled from a living body. *Living machinery. Modified tissue...*

The biological truth of it was too heavy for a Luminary's logic to bear. My reasoning shattered.

I threw myself into the data-drainage conduit. My body — throttled to 0.75x — made everything around me agonizingly slow, the scan-light sweeping in close behind.

The toe of my right boot cleared the lip just in time. The scan-light grazed only the heel.

A sensation of *nothing* shot up through my ankle as I fell. When I glanced down in the plummeting dark, my right heel was simply gone. Not torn, not burned — deleted. The model data had ceased to exist. The wireframe remained — a blinking skeletal outline, searching for texture data it would never receive again.

I tumbled into the cold dark, letting myself fall through the drainage network, leaving the light of erasure above.

Carrying the virus. The secret key. And the memory of blood and flesh — lodged deep in digital grey matter.
