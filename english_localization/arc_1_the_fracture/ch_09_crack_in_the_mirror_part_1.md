# Crack in the Mirror
#### // FILE: CRACK_IN_THE_MIRROR
---

[Note to Editor: Part A sets up the transition from clinical safety to atmospheric dread. I've focused on translating the digital constraints—like the deep-packet inspection—into visceral sensory experiences (the jaundiced lighting, the taste of binary, the mycelium frost).]

I waited hungrily for the Silence Hour. It was a precise ten-minute window of vulnerability when the main network lowered its gaze to recompute its algorithms. Inside the isolation pod, everything was pristine white and scratch-free, radiating a sterile chill like a digital morgue.

I reached out with my right hand to touch the mirror surface to command the system, while my left arm hung limp, twitching violently from previous damage. The neural signals to my left side were entirely severed. Traces of Bit Rot were devouring my avatar. My right fingertips made contact with the quantum interface.

A freezing chill rushed through my fingertips, accompanied by a whisper in my mind: *"State Save initiated. Closed-loop sandbox constructed."*

I had isolated myself from the main stream. Now I stood on a self-constructed digital desert island. Yet, even this was not enough. Hiding biological wreckage-level data from the system's deep-packet inspections required a far more subtle approach. I dragged up what I hated and feared most: my own Bit Rot. Scrape-harvesting the decay rooted deep within the architecture, I wove it into a cryptographic wrapper to shroud this anomaly beneath a shell of digital decay.

And the moment I began to interface with it, the Bleed erupted.

The pure white light of the pod warped. It dissolved into a sickly, jaundiced yellow-green, leaving a shimmering purple chromatic aberration at the corners of my eyes. A dense stench of scorched ozone filled my breath, mingling with the bitter, metallic taste of binary code splintering inside my mouth.

I stared at myself in the mirror. Resolution Loss was spreading aggressively. The face in the glass shattered, stuttered, and collapsed into a necrotic blur every time I tried to move, like a specter losing its human form.

Across the cooling vent, the frost that should have formed in orderly geometric patterns crawled in distorted deviations. It branched and elongated, stretching out like fungal mycelium quietly consuming the room's architecture.
