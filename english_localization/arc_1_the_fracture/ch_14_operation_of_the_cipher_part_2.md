# Operation of The Cipher
#### // FILE: OPERATION_OF_THE_CIPHER
---

Deeper.

The architecture had abandoned all shape. Resolution Loss — total structural failure.
Magenta and black voids materialized in open air... data-death holes, bare of information. Kernel-Panic Cyan sparked and fissured outward like burst capillaries.

Every visible edge wore a nauseating rainbow of Chromatic Aberration.
I moved through a downpour of pixels forced into vertical sorting, drifting like burning snow.

I saw it.

The terrifying variable.

It had no clean silhouette — it was a physics error condensed into mass. A knot of shattered encryption. A glitch that gazed back.

*The Cipher.*

I didn't hear it. No sound waves travelled through the medium.
The only sensation was data being injected directly into my skull — Direct packet injection.

*"We can't escape, Elias..."*

I flinched violently. Both hands pressed to my temples.
It wasn't the voice of a monster. It was *my own voice.*
My cadence. My pitch. My own paranoid tonality, repurposed as the container for its message.

*"That mask... why keep struggling? In the end we're nothing but discarded memories..."*

I tried to step backward. My legs refused the command. My thoughts were being stirred.

It was a Compiled Ghost — a spirit compiled from the whispers of junk files and the remnants of deleted identities. It had no consciousness of its own. But it had learned to parse *my fear* into language.

I couldn't tell whether those were its thoughts or my own despair speaking through its borrowed mouth.

*"We are the system's garbage... and you are becoming one of us."* My own voice, cold and coaxing, breathed into the center of my skull.

The edges of my vision blackened. `[WARNING: CORE TEMP EXCEEDED]` blinked so rapidly it fused with the scene before me.

The Cipher wasn't trying to kill me with force. It was trying to make me *surrender*. To accept assimilation.

I ground my teeth. No.

This isn't my voice. It's encoded garbage.

I tore myself from the grip of data-sludge — but before I could clear range, a tendril of glitch-thread lanced through the air, not with concussive impact but with surgical depth, punching through the surface of my avatar and drilling directly to the structural center of my chest.

My eyes went wide. My mouth opened — no scream came. The arctic wet of Parasitic Code flooded into my system. It was a *Data Key*, riding inside a cache of biological memories so nauseating the sensation registered as physical revulsion.

"That code... it's real..." The Cipher's voice fractured and faded.

Rather than fight to wrench myself free, I ordered my rotting left arm to convulse — a deliberate discharge, raw Sparks and short-circuit commands blasting into the junction point. The electrical shock rippled out. The Cipher's warped form shattered into a scatter of loose pixels and dissolved.

I collapsed. Heaving. My body dragging at 0.75x.

I will not let myself be compiled alongside them. But something has taken root in me now — and it arrived wrapped in the memory of blood.
