# The Deletion Sweep
#### // FILE: THE_DELETION_SWEEP
---

The Compiled Ghost was gone. What remained was a dead weight of emptiness and the Data Key now rooted inside my consciousness.

It had no clean binary architecture — nothing elegant about what The Cipher had forced into my skull. It was *wet*. It felt like digital parasites writhing and gnawing at logic from the inside. This data was raw, feral, and it carried a concept my 32K architecture simply could not process: *tissue... blood... biological servers.*

I dropped to both knees on the melting concrete, craned forward, tried to retch out something that didn't exist. The body's attempt to simulate violent rejection. Amber UI warning windows strobed rapidly across my field of view.

`[WARNING: UNKNOWN PAYLOAD DETECTED. CORE LOGIC COMPROMISED.]`

But before I could begin to address the chaos within, the low groan of hardware being ground apart in the Slag-Tiers was split by a new sound — a piercing, high, crystalline shriek. Clean. Precise. Cold as a diamond cutting glass.

The Deletion Sweep had begun.

Far out at the edges of my vision, through the smog of red-pixel haze and heavy shadow, a wall of pure white light was advancing — contracting inward. Zero-Kelvin Blue. The light of The Archive's perfection, sent down to purify.

I scrambled backward on my heels. When that wall of light swept through the Brutalist ruins — through the sagging, decaying architecture — what it did was not immediate obliteration. It was something far more cruel. The instant the light touched a structure, it was force-rendered: rebuilt in perfect 32K marble and clean stone in a single instant. Then the system's logic would catch up, recognize the underlying mass as garbage, and delete the entire building into pure `#000000` void. Forever.

No ash. No rubble. Only immaculate, irreversible Deletion.

`[ALERT: ASYNCHRONOUS BATCH PROCESSING INITIATED. EVACUATE ZONE.]`

I had to run. Every instinct screamed it. But my body was locked — throttled to 0.75x by Thermal Throttling. The air boiled, thick and heavy. My depth perception had been stripped by the system to conserve the last dregs of processing power, rendering the world in flat 2D.

Every step was adhesion — red data-sludge grabbing at my boots like boiling tar, attempting to overwrite the soles as I wrenched each foot free. The scene before me bent and twisted in Chromatic Aberration as I lurched toward the cooling tower's silhouette.

The white light was close behind. The screams of data being deleted echoed in my auditory channels. The garbage code around me attempted to flee the light the way insects flee a flame. I wasn't merely escaping death — I was fleeing permanent erasure from the very substrate of the simulation. Not deletion of my character. Deletion of my *existence* as a computational fact.
