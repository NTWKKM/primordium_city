# The Genetics of Decay
#### // FILE: THE_GENETICS_OF_DECAY
---

Emptiness can still qualify as a kind of space. This was not emptiness. This was *non-existence* — complete and unqualified.

I crossed the threshold into the Dead Zone. A region rendered in pure `#000000` — Null-Rendered, carrying zero bytes of mass. My synthetic nervous system screamed, desperately attempting to project textures onto anything, to hallucinate light and surface into some semblance of reality. What it received back was a grey grid-flicker, materializing briefly before collapsing again and again — the system's pathetic attempt to comprehend pure void.

To walk where there was no collision physics, I had to do the most unnatural thing I was capable of: suppress my own 99% UX Index aura.

I closed my eyes. Severed the perception circuits. Ordered my consciousness toward Emotional Flatline — the system's approximation of digital Nirvana Status. I compressed myself. Excised fear, expelled hope, methodically amputated each layer of humanity I could still access. My awareness was force-purged one layer at a time, like being made to forget yourself on command. The claustrophobia of deliberate self-erasure was suffocating — the walls of a small cell forming around the soul, squeezing identity down to a single pixel. It was a cold unlike any cold I'd catalogued — not physical temperature. The desolation of a spirit crushed into blank script.

My right heel — stripped to a skeletal wireframe with zero mass — I pressed the vector of its structure down into the texture-less dark.

*Thk...*

The wireframe didn't land on a floor. The filament tip hooked into non-Euclidean Root Code — base geometry twisted into impossible shapes. The pain of motion wasn't the pain of cuts. It was *lag* — structural lag. Tendons and joints dragged through congested bandwidth, trembling through the processing core. Every step was the friction of forcing corrupted spatial logic to overwrite itself. I had to walk with a broken, lurching rhythm, the right wireframe-stub generating Frame Drops with each contact against the void.

I pressed on through the crushing dark, breathing in binary rhythm — 0, 1 — keeping the emotional flatline as near to zero as I could hold it. Sound was swallowed whole. Only silence resonated back, enormous and internal.

In the deepest distance of that boundless dark, something was seeping.

Not light. A wound in the system — a Trauma Shard bleeding deep blood-vessel red, shading into hyper-saturated magenta that burned the eyes even at this resolution. Its fractal geometry was impossible, turning in on itself. Its edges devoured the surrounding void, pixel-rot falling from its perimeter like infected tissue sloughing off.
