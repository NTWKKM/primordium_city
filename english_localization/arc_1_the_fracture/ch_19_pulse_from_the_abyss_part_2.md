# Pulse from the Abyss
#### // FILE: PULSE_FROM_THE_ABYSS
---

The marble wall stood immaculate, seamless, flawless. The shadowless white light compressed me into a single blemish on an otherwise perfect world. The Nullifiers rewrote their coordinates in a ring around every vector of escape. Their fractal rings hummed at a frequency that made my synthetic nervous system feel like it was shaking itself apart.

If I maintained 32K status, they would lock the target and dissolve me into null.

I had to surrender the perfection.

My corrupted fingertips spasmed. I hauled the decayed arm up by will alone, forcing my subconscious to accept ruin. I issued the command to scale down my own processing — to embrace Resolution Loss. To cut myself loose from the anchor of high fidelity.

*Release.*
*Diminish.*
*Tear away.*

My rendering architecture cracked. Textures compressed to smears. The polygons comprising my body contracted, warped, and turned coarse. The sensation was being skinned — the pain of losing the high-resolution shielding hit without warning, my body becoming nothing but a confused mass of shivering, rotting data. Stuttering spread through every motion. Each step that pressed the wireframe stub down triggered Screen Tearing in the scene around me.

I forced my left arm — bent, warping from constant Artifacting — into a rigid arc and drove it at full force into the 32K marble wall.

*Crack.*
*Glitch.*

The arm didn't shatter. The Trauma Shard injected raw biological chaos directly into The Archive's architecture. Its algorithms attempted to compute, to render, to resolve *human pain*. They couldn't. The system's logic was built for perfection only. It had no equation for genuine torment.

The perfect wall suffered a Segmentation Fault. The high-end marble froze mid-render, then collapsed into a mosaic of green and purple blocks — the geometry's code unraveling into a Clipping Error, a void opening downward into undefined space beneath.

The Nullifiers blinked into close range, closing the net. They were too slow.

I didn't flee by hiding among garbage data — The Archive sweeps everything. Instead I chose the only exit the system hadn't accounted for: I stepped off the edge of the defined world.

I fell through the gap. Through the crumbling geometry. Out of bounds. Beyond the coordinate space The Archive could reach.

My low-poly, half-dissolved body plunged into an unfathomable dark. Above, chromium ring-light tried helplessly to overwrite the breach, sealing the hole behind me.

I was gone into the space the simulation had forgotten to make impossible.
