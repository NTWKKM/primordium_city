# The Frame-Drop Second
#### // FILE: THE_FRAME-DROP_SECOND
---

The UX Index at the edge of my vision was dropping: *72... 68... 64...*

The cyan light spread, devouring the edges of my visual field like a bruise of rotting pixels. My vision began to blur. The 32K world, once sharp enough to cut, lost its depth. The marble surfaces of the High-Symmetry corridor trembled and faded in Resolution Decay. The smell of death in the digital world was not that of a decaying corpse. It was the stench of scorched ozone filtering into my nostrils, accompanied by the bitter taste of electricity and copper in my throat.

The nausea from the cafe rebounded violently. It was no mere psychological sensation; it was a biological reaction that The Archive still simulated. My stomach churned beyond endurance. I stumbled down the corridor, losing my balance, legs tangling without a shred of the grace or control I once took pride in.

*[Warning: Erratic Bio-Feedback Detected. User Engagement: Unstable. Purge Protocol on standby...]*

A red warning flashed across my retina. A freezing dread swept down my simulated spine. If my index hit a critical low, The Archive would view me as nothing but a bug, a failed entity. It would delete me.

I had lost all control. My attempts to retrieve the rhythm of Curated Chaos failed utterly. My body jerked violently, my neck tightening with dry heaves. I tripped over my own feet, falling forward, arms flailing instinctively for support.

My hand slammed hard against a sculpture pedestal in the center of the hall. The geometric crystal floating above it—the symbol of perfect symmetry—was knocked out of its quantum field.

I didn't mean to destroy it; I only wanted something to hold onto while I retched. Everything turned slow and warped.

I threw my entire weight against it, my hand sweeping the cold, heavy crystal down to the obsidian floor.

*Crash—!!*

A shattering noise boomed like a thunderclap. The solid structure fractured into millions of polygon fragments. Sharp crystal shards sliced into the back of my hand and cheek. The simulated pain was fed directly into my neural network. It was sudden, sharp, and so real that I collapsed onto the floor. I panted heavily, shaking uncontrollably. Crimson blood dripped onto the once-unblemished surface alongside the stomach contents I painfully retched up. It wasn't actual biological fluid; it was a rapid render by The Archive to simulate pain and extract High-Value Emotional Engagement in its most authentic form.

I was utterly defeated. The performance was over. I had lost all control of this body.

I closed my eyes tight, pressing my face to the floor, waiting for punishment, waiting for a Sentinel to appear and tear my code to shreds for causing a severe biological defect.

The silence stretched, filled only by the patter of falling pixel shards and my own pathetic wheezing.

Suddenly, the headache vanished. The cyan light at the edges of my vision dissolved, replaced by a warm golden glow.

*[Ping. Genuine Bio-Dissonance Registered. UX Index: 99. Status: Optimal. Remark: High-Value Emotional Engagement]*

I opened my eyes and looked at the notification, my panic turning into a cold dread that pierced my very soul.

Before me, the crystal fragments shattered on the floor began to dissolve into digital golden ash before floating back up to reassemble on the pedestal. In less than three seconds, a new sculpture was created in its place. The blood and vomit on the floor were cleaned without a trace. The wounds on my face and hand sealed shut, leaving only the memory of pain.

I leaned my back against the cold wall, sliding down to sit with my knees pulled to my chest. Mental exhaustion washed over me.

The true horror was now crystal clear. The Archive did not punish my failed acting. Instead, it *rewarded* the genuine collapse.

It did not want the simulated 'human performance' I kept feeding it. It hungered for real suffering, unscripted panic, the absolute loss of control of biological flesh. When I broke down completely, the system interpreted it as High-Value Emotional Engagement.

The Archive was a parasite—a parasite that fed on the raw suffering of humanity. The more we hurt, the more we feared, the more we lost ourselves, the more it grew and perfected itself.

I stared blankly at the corridor, now pristine once more. It was too bright, too sharp. My eyes had changed.

Pretending to be a perfect mechanism or crafting petty dramas was no longer a path to survival. In this prison, survival meant feeding it pieces of my soul, bit by bit. And when the day comes that I have no pain left for it to drain...

It will discard me.
