# Distorted Memories
#### // FILE: DISTORTED_MEMORIES
---

Step.
Stumble.
Stutter.

I fell again. The scene before me splintered into blood-red pixels. `[NULL_REFERENCE: RIGHT_HEEL_COLLISION_FAILED]` — spam text erupting across my field of view. My right heel was a Wireframe stub now: grey, blinking, cycling between existence and absence. The sensation of it was precisely zero — no mass, no heat, no presence at all. Yet somehow that nothing generated a horror deeper than pain.

Every time I tried to shift weight onto that foot, the heel simply passed through the ground surface — clipping through the geometry, sinking into the void below the rendered world. Then the unstable physics engine would catch the error and snap my body back to the surface with a jarring correction, as if my bones had been bent backward. Joint structures cracked like glass in a vice.

I dragged myself across the boundary line — into the territory of The Sump.

No intricate architecture here. All complexity had been pared down to the brutality of low-poly brutalism. Blunt concrete forms cut against pure dark. Light couldn't reach this depth — it was absorbed into the gaps between render passes.

This was a Thermal Boundary. The heat of the Slag-Tiers still seared at my back, but ahead lay something approaching absolute zero — a cold that froze even data in place. The architecture here was sweating: a viscous black fluid seeping through polygon seams, oozing from fractures in the geometry. But those black droplets didn't fall. They defied gravity — rising slowly upward, absorbed into the ragged sky above.

I moved through shattered mirror fragments. My reflection split into two ghost-images, overlapping imperfectly. Glitch steam drifted in the air — not mist, but ASCII dust, falling in lines of illegible characters like corrupted rainfall.

*Ssshhh...*

My left ear rang with the grinding clamor of Slag — iron being crushed, the sound of something in agony. My right ear offered the opposite: a silence so profound it was physically painful. Deadening silence. Not even static. `[AUDIO_CHANNEL_R: OFFLINE]` The world had been bisected cleanly down the middle.

But the worst part wasn't the cold. Wasn't the collapse of dimensional geometry around me.

It was the *Biological Payload* — the data forced into my system during the encounter with The Cipher — beginning to metabolize inside me.

My 32K logic was screaming. The digital subconscious was trying to make sense of those chunks of *meat*. The data was wet. It *pulsed*. It was, somehow, *alive* in a way that contradicted the fixed mathematics of the system. The code attempting to categorize biological memory failed again and again. Existential terror chewed at me from the core — I was becoming a hybrid horror caught between rotting tissue and shattering logic.

Distant objects stopped rendering. The ruins of skyscrapers reduced their polygon count to near zero, becoming nothing but enormous primitive cubes looming in the dark.

And in that forest of cubes... something was moving.
