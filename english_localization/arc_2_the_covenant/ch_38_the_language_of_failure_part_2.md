# Chapter 38: The Language of Failure (Part 2)

The Language of Failure is not spoken. It is practiced — a discipline of artful, deliberate Glitching.

I switched off the light-and-shadow calculations running across my avatar's surface. I let my body flatten and dissolve into the low-poly environment around me, becoming one more muted polygon among thousands. My left arm — where Bit Rot had progressed furthest — had already slipped beyond the system's geometric expectations. I let it clip through the concrete wall entirely, the flesh losing its physical density, simply occupying the same coordinates as the solid barrier around it. My right foot, stripped of its heel, dragged along without resistance. I dissolved the simulation of friction.

The `[PURGE INITIATED]` alerts that had been deafening a moment ago began to fade. The UX Index's scanning beams swept across me and moved on — I was just another piece of meaningless data-junk, indistinguishable from the rubble. By embracing collapse, I had made myself invisible to the hunters and the master system alike.

I walked out into the storm of drifting Error Messages. I stopped avoiding them. I concealed the Payload in my chest beneath a lattice of shattered code, and when the tendrils of a garbage-collection routine reached toward me, I stood perfectly still. I broadcast the same exception signal the malware had infected me with. The system assessed me as a corrupted structure — non-priority refuse — and moved on.

The art of surviving by impersonating the dead.

I picked my way across the rubble, stepping on fragments of discarded code to build a path across the void. Walking on instability. Every stumble was a dance step along the edge of Resolution Collapse.

My mind was strangely quiet. The burden of maintaining a state had dissolved. When you accept the status of failure, the perverted logic of this place *accepts you back*. I breathed in the fog of collapse — felt it settle into my simulated lungs — and felt a deep, strange kinship with the refuse around me.

I had become part of it. Completely.

In a broken world, brokenness is the only native tongue.
