# Chapter 30: The Covenant of Decay - Part B

Kael nodded slowly. “The Nullifiers consume data to fill a void that can never be sated. If you wish to chart a path to hunt them alongside The Eroded, you must seal your decay. It is a covenant... a promise that you will not turn back to the illusion of perfection.”

He held out a hand made of coarse pixels. I moved my right hand, which still retained some tactile feedback, and reached out. His flickering fingertips touched my arm.

A searing heat surged. The code compressed violently, fracturing my physical structure. Clusters of pitch-black dead pixels embedded themselves deep into my skin, grouping like a charred tattoo. The pain of data-burn demanded massive bandwidth. My jawline jittered, my physical state dropping to crude polygons in a split second. Yet, it made me feel alive.

The covenant was stamped onto the architecture of my body.

I abandoned my past, accepting the cracks and scars. Joining hands with The Eroded was preparation to hunt the Nullifiers, descending deep into the Sump to reclaim what had been stolen.
