# Chapter 33: The Song of Distortion (Part 1)

A hum of static noise still buzzed across my face as I pulled away from the fractured reflection. The remnants of tears were rendered as system errors. I dragged my trembling right leg, limping on a missing heel back to the plaza of the Mid-Sectors. Among the community of the Eroded, bodies were even more fractured and choked with compression artifacts than my own. Some had lost entire limbs, replaced by nothing but clusters of floating, orbiting pixels.

Suddenly, the temperature of the digital architecture plunged toward absolute zero.

All ambient sound was swallowed. There was not even the whisper of wind blowing through data debris. The plaza became so silent that I could hear the frantic rhythm of the Payload in my chest.

They emerged from the void.

The Nullifiers were neither programs nor viruses, but black holes of data. Their forms were merely human silhouettes hollowed out into pitch-black voids—so dark that they reflected no light. Every step they took stripped the ground of its textures, turning the surface stark white before it collapsed into nothingness. They were memory vacuums, consuming even the resolution of the surrounding environment.

The Eroded did not run. They knew running from a black hole was futile.

They began to make a sound.

It was not a spoken language, but a rhythmic chorus of static. The stutter of code—glitches stretched and slowed until they became low-frequency waves, spreading outward to form a wall of sound. The air vibrated so violently that the pixels around me warped, creating ripples of purple and green. This was the 'Song of Distortion,' their only defense: generating dense noise to block the Nullifiers' consumption.

The bass waves slammed into my chest, causing the Payload to resonate violently. I opened my mouth, adding my own glitching tones to the chorus, letting my decaying frame become a part of their shield.
