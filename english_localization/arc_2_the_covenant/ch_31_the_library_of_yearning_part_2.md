# Chapter 31: The Library of Yearning (Part 2)

I could not resist the pull. My scarred right hand trembled as I reached out to touch a cube on a shelf at eye level. My left arm, eaten away by Bit Rot, spasmed in sync with the pulse of data.

The moment my fingertips brushed the smooth surface, the external world vanished.

The scent of wet earth after rain. The cool touch of water droplets. The warmth of intertwined palms. Resonant laughter and a feeling of 'love' so intense it threatened to split my chest open... it was a fraction of a second, preserved forever in pristine 32K resolution.

I yanked my hand back, losing my balance and collapsing to my knees, gasping frantically. The memory was far too intense for my crumbling structure to sustain. My body flickered, blinking in and out, reduced to crude blocks of grey pixels. Resolution Loss flared to its limit as I struggled to process the pain.

It was not my memory, but it was a reflection of what I had lost. I tried to wring a sense of love from my own past, yet all that returned was data compressed to the point of numbness. I could no longer truly feel it.

My system discarded complexity to survive, and so the sadness carving deep into my core became nothing more than a flat, barren sensation. The golden light shone upon my fractured face, exposing the ugliness buried in my depths. I knelt before this graveyard of remembrance, letting yearning consume my blurring self.
