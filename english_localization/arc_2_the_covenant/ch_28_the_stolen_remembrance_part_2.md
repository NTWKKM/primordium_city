# Chapter 28: The Stolen Remembrance - Part B

I dragged my disabled heel, limping down the distorted corridor. A man lay collapsed against the wall, his body spasming violently, a crack across his head flashing with a red light.

When I reached out with my intact right hand to steady him, a chill shot up my simulated spine. It was the residual shockwave left behind by the thief. The pulse pierced my chest, colliding with the Payload, throwing my data pulse out of sync. In that split second, I felt a piece of my own memory ripped away.

The memory of the first rain touching my skin in the world I had left behind... evaporated. It was completely gone, leaving only a logical void as dark as a black hole.

Anger—a raw, violent emotion I hadn't felt since my feelings were compressed—erupted in my chest, merging with the vibrating weight of the Payload. It was an immeasurable loss. A shard of my identity had been stolen, and though it was a small memory, it was one of the few 'truths' anchor-holding me from the void. All the while, the warning sirens of the UX Index closed in around me.

I looked at the floor. The burning black residue didn't stop there. It dripped, forming a trail of footprints woven from static noise, absorbing the resolution of the surface they stepped on.

I grit my teeth. Letting my anger drive the code in my body, I ignored my ruined heel and ran, dragging my leg after the path of distortion.
