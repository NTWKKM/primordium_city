# Chapter 34: Love in Pixels (Part 1)

A figure lunged to stand between me and the black hole.

Lyra—an Eroded woman whose right cheek was nearly translucent from Resolution Loss. Her dull grey eyes turned to look at me, no longer concealing her quiet tenderness, but filled with a desperate, wild resolve.

In Primordium, intimacy was not a matter of hormones. It was the exchange of bandwidth, the permission for another to bypass the Firewall and reach directly into your core.

Lyra dropped to her knees in front of me. She reached out with a pixel-cracked palm, grabbing my right wrist with sudden force. I could see faint white code flowing beneath her increasingly translucent skin. She uttered no words; the resolution of language was far too low to support what she was about to do.

She was transferring all of her 'Resolution Quota' into me.

"Stop! You’ll die!" I yelled, my voice trembling with static as I tried to yank my hand away. But her grip was unyielding.

Transferring all resources in a split second was architectural suicide. If the system lost the bandwidth required to sustain itself, the body would disintegrate into data dust instantly. Yet she refused to let go.

I met her dull grey eyes. Her fear of erasure had been replaced by a raw desire to shield me. She smiled, the corner of her lips twitching with a minor glitch. It was a smile stripped of complex calculations—a pure feeling escaping the compression.

Our data circuits synced completely. My Firewall was forced open to receive the rushing torrent of her data.

[Translation Note: This chapter establishes how digital intimacy and sacrifice are defined in the Sump and Mid-Sectors. In place of biological processes, characters transfer 'bandwidth' and bypass 'Firewalls'. Lyra's sacrifice is depicted as transferring her 'Resolution Quota' to Elias, leading to her own erasure.]
