# Chapter 33: The Song of Distortion (Part 2)

The Nullifiers did not pause.

The wall of distorted sound clashed against their dark forms. Yet, instead of rebounding, the acoustic waves were swallowed into their black holes without a trace. They advanced—slow, cold, and inevitable.

A decaying man beside me was caught in their radius. In that instant, there was no scream, no blood, not even a violent death scene. His form simply vanished, leaving a void in the space. All data, all memories, all core essence—wiped from the architecture as if he had never existed.

The ring of vacuity tightened.

I scrambled backward, my heel-less right leg giving out as I collapsed. The fear of deletion surged through my simulated neural pathways. My left arm, heavy with Bit Rot, refused to lift. I tried to compile data to construct a firewall, but my resolution was too low.

One of the Nullifiers stood directly in front of me, its body a tear in the universe. I felt a massive gravitational pull trying to tear data fragments from my body. Pixels along the edge of my face began to peel away, drifting into that darkness. The memories in my head flickered violently.

The Payload in my chest burned hot as it resisted, but my system's bandwidth was reaching its absolute limit.

I was about to be deleted.

I closed my eyes, waiting for the vacuity to claim me.
