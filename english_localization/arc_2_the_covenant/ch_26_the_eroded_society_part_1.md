# Chapter 26: The Eroded Society - Part 1

I limped across the boundary of the Mid-Sectors, leaving the hard, sterile blue light of the Wardens behind. My missing right heel made every step sway, the rough surface scraping against my simulated sole, creating the grating hiss of splintering pixels. The air grew heavier, as if weighted down by unprocessed data mass. The smell of moldy code drifted through the air—a sharp sting of burnt ozone mixed with damp paper.

This was the margin of digital consciousness, a borderland where The Archive had ceased allocating resources for repairs. Inside my mind, my UX Index flashed; the hunting algorithms were still searching for my traces.

The scene before me was a community built from the corpses of the simulation. Buildings were patched together from collapsed structures, rough grey polygons fused by dim, flickering code lines that looked like pulseless veins.

I saw them—The Eroded.

The citizens here lacked the smooth skin of the Inner Ring. Their bodies were stained with the scars of compression, distorted patches of green and purple clinging to their skin like burn marks. Their clothing, woven from torn pixels, jittered at the edges before dissolving into digital ash that floated in the air.

I threw my weight onto my trembling left leg, looking down at my hands. My right arm was still high-resolution, while my left was so consumed by Bit Rot that it was numb, sending sharp, tiny spikes of pain like short-circuiting electricity.

The Payload in my chest throbbed with the pulse of data. It was heavy, forcing me to hunch over, yet it served as a reminder that I was still alive.

I inhaled the noise-laden air, its grit slicing deep through my throat. There was no perfection here, only decaying reality—and it might be the closest thing to human in this mirrored city.
