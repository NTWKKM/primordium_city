# Chapter 25: Whispers from the Artifacts - Part A

The sky above had lost its depth. It was flat, the blue vault replaced by a shallow grid, like a painting recorded over so many times that its details shattered. I dragged my heel-less right foot across the borders of the Mid-Sectors, every step an unbalanced distortion.

I collapsed against a brick wall, its pattern running like wet watercolor. I forced my left arm up to look at it. The spread of compression artifacts was stark: patches of fluorescent purple and green embedded deep beneath my simulated skin. A dull numbness rippled alongside intense pain. In the center of my chest, the Data Key Payload throbbed—a heavy pendulum reminding me that the Hounds were still unleashing their algorithms to hunt me down without pause.

My eyes stared at the decay on my forearm, etching it deep into my consciousness. I had once feared this stigma; it was a decree of failure, a steep cliff waiting to plunge me into the void of The Hollows.

Yet, in the hours away from the hard, sterile light of the Preservationists, I noticed something.

Those colored patches did not flicker randomly.

They had a rhythm.

I closed my eyes, focusing my senses to grasp the 'sound' of that erosion. Normally, Bit Rot sent out a static hiss like a television with no signal. But when my consciousness synced with the fracture, those sounds peeled back, separating clearly into distinct frequency bands.
