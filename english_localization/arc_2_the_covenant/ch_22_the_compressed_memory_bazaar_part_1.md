# Chapter 22: The Compressed Memory Bazaar - Part A

The first sound that struck my ears as I dragged my mangled heels into the central plaza of the Mid-Sectors was a warped, discordant din. I pressed my left arm, bloated from Bit Rot, close to my side. My right hand clutched my chest, concealing the bulk of the Data Key Payload beneath my shirt. My eyes remained alert for the Hounds, who might be tracking a High UX signal, lurking in the crowd.

It wasn’t a lively, human clamor. The ambient sound was a low, pitch-shifted hum. Conversations dragged, interrupted by bursts of static like a radio failing to lock onto a station. The plaza was a tangled mess of digital steel and concrete. Red and blue neon light from shop signs melted and dripped into the grey mist, resembling a watercolor painting lashed by acid rain.

This was the Memory Black Market—the economic hub of a district where every square inch was downscaled.

Hundreds of stalls were packed along the cracked streets. No one sold food or clothing. In a world where the body was nothing but a cluster of code, such things were meaningless. What lined the rusted iron tables were "Data Cubes"—translucent geometric blocks with dim flickers of light trapped at their centers.

I walked closer to one of the stalls. The vendor was a gaunt, spindly man whose upper torso was riddled with the purple-bruised gaps of compression artifacts. He glanced at me with eyes that were merely flat white slits, devoid of pupils.

“You look like you just dropped down, kid,” his voice sputtered in intervals. “Your resolution still carries the fresh scent of the Inner Ring... Care to make a trade?”

“Trade what?” I asked, my own voice beginning to vibrate with instability.

He picked up an amber cube and rolled it across his palm. “This is the *feeling of a first kiss*. Medium resolution, a little stutter at the end... but the butterflies in the stomach are fully intact. I'll trade it for one of your high-res memories. Something with a full, vivid blue sky. I'll give you a good price.”

My eyes widened as I stared at the glowing cube in his hand.

High-resolution memories... they were the true currency of this hell.

To keep themselves from degrading into The Hollows—those mindless data shells trapped in loop states—the people here had to consume vivid feelings to stimulate their own algorithms. They carved out pieces of their own past to sell, buying time, purchasing others' memories to patch up their own fraying, breaking identities.

I quickly limped away from the stall, a wave of nausea rising in my throat. The oppressive weight of the system's gaze still pressed against the back of my neck. Packaging and bargaining over the most fragile human emotions... it was cruder and more vicious than physical murder.
