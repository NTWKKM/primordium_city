# Chapter 25: Whispers from the Artifacts - Part B

It wasn't just empty static. It was a language.

The rapid, stuttering pulses melded into a strange percussion. The low-frequency hum hid a deep, sorrowful melody. It was a hymn of distortion... whispers from code rejected by The Archive.

*“...Sink... sink down below the glass...”*

My eyes snapped open. The voice did not penetrate my eardrums; it rooted itself directly in my neural pathways, transmitted from the compression artifacts on my left arm.

I forced myself up, balancing precariously on my disabled leg. I scanned the wreckage before me. This decaying data was no digital trash; it was a compressed cipher, hidden by someone lurking at the very bottom of the Sump.

The Preservationists feared decay because they believed it was the end. They were wrong. Artifacting was a rusted key, unlocking hidden doors to the truth buried beneath the rug of fake perfection.

My right hand clutched the payload in my chest, while my fingers traced the vibrating purple patches on my left arm. I felt the pulse of a mutating code. The dread of the Hounds' fangs evaporated, replaced by a consuming curiosity.

If low resolution was the price of seeing the truth... I was ready to let myself blur.
