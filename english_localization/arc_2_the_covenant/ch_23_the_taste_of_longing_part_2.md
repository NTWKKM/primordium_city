# Chapter 23: The Taste of Longing - Part B

“You look like someone searching for loose change in a torn pocket.”

The voice was slightly pitch-shifted, trembling like radio interference. I looked up to see a wandering memory peddler approaching. He was a man in a dull grey coat, the hem of his garment stuttering every time he moved, as if captured in a low-framerate video.

“I have a *first kiss in the rain*, imported from the Inner Ring,” he offered, turning his palm to reveal a glowing Data Cube radiating a faint blue light. “8K resolution. Guaranteed wetness of raindrops and a heart rate spiking past 120 beats per minute. Interested? A single childhood memory of yours is all it takes.”

I stared at the cube. Driven by yearning, I reflexively reached out my left arm. My fingertips, scarred with the ragged gaps of Bit Rot, touched its glass surface to preview it... The connection was sluggish, marred by static and stutters.

Suddenly, the image of a girl beneath a clear umbrella flashed in my mind. But instead of a profound touch, my system buckled under a violent stutter. Her face splintered into square mosaics. The contact against my lips felt neither warm nor soft; it was rough and rigid, like pressing my face against the glass of a cold television screen.

I yanked my aching left arm back, gasping, as a sharp pang throbbed in my chest where the Data Key hung heavy. The oppressive presence of the hunting Hounds remained at my neck like a frozen blade.

It wasn't that his merchandise was defective. It was *me*.

My emotional bandwidth had deteriorated to the point where I could no longer process such intricate feelings. Even if I spent everything to buy the most perfect love, my system would compress it down into a flat, dimensionless satisfaction anyway.

“No...” I refused, my voice hoarse. “It... it doesn’t work on me anymore.”

The peddler shrugged, pocketing the cube without pushing further, leaving me with the silence and the polygonal apple that was beginning to dissolve into pixel dust on the table. The true horror of decay is not the loss of memories. The horror is realizing you have permanently lost the *ability to feel* them.
