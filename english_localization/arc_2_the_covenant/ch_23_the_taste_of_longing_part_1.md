# Chapter 23: The Taste of Longing - Part A

Sometimes I forgot what real human hunger was like.

In the Mid-Sectors, hunger usually manifested as a dim notification blinking at the periphery of my vision, like a silent low-battery warning. I dragged my limping body to rest at a table in a rundown diner on a street corner, hiding my left arm—bloated and rotting with Bit Rot—underneath the table. I breathed with difficulty under the heavy constriction of the Data Key in my chest. My eyes scanned the room, paranoid that the Hounds would track my High UX signature. The tabletop, which had once simulated a delicate oak grain, had now lost its texture, decaying into a flat, uniform brown—a victim of texture loss that looked like watercolor smeared with a wet sponge.

I ordered a digital apple, hoping only to trigger old memory codes. I tried to pick it up, but my infected left arm was so numb and spasming uncontrollably that I nearly dropped it. I had to force my trembling right hand to take it instead. The apple was too perfect in shape—no bruises, no blemishes. When I bit into it... there was no crisp snap, no sweet juice seeping past my teeth. There was only the sensation of a polygon breaking apart, and a flat, repetitive taste, like chewing cardboard coated in a thin layer of artificial sugar.

It was satisfaction downscaled to the absolute minimum.

I closed my eyes, trying to dig into my personal database—my own archive. Searching for memories of 'love'. I remembered vaguely that there had been someone... someone whose hand I once held beneath the warm lights of the Inner Ring. But when I tried to pull that feeling up, I received no warm flutter in my chest, no racing heart.

My system was undergoing severe emotional compression.

Complex love, the dimensions of infatuation, the terror of loss... all of it was squeezed and compressed into a single, flat state: **"Satisfied"**. It was like ticking a box on a form. *Do you love her? Yes.*

Logic said yes. Yet, there was no flavor to it, no weight, not even the pain of longing.

I opened my eyes, watching the pixels at the edge of the apple begin to detach and blur into the surrounding air. The musty scent of compressed data drifted to my nose, like ozone mixed with iron dust. This was the taste of longing in Primordium... yearning for something you know exists, but no longer having the resolution to feel.
