# Chapter 31: The Library of Yearning (Part 1)

Kael led me through the rotting architecture of the Fringes. Data vibrations resonated deep within my core essence. I dragged my jagged, pixelated right leg behind him, every step a struggle against warped gravity. The surrounding walls peeled away into flat, matte-grey polygons. The atmosphere grew hushed, as though enveloped in a dense tide of secrets.

We broke through a cloaking veil of overlapping error messages. The sight before us forced my simulated breathing cycles to stutter.

A colossal library, devoid of geometric rules, soared into the darkness. Spiral staircases floated suspended in mid-air. What filled the shelves were not books, but hundreds of thousands—millions—of Data Cubes, emitting a soft, golden glow.

"The Nullifiers herd memories and store them here," Kael whispered. "This is their nest… a graveyard of the past."

I extended my right hand—the one branded with dead pixels—into the air. The scar throbbed, reacting to the massive swell of data. The smooth finish of the golden cubes stood in stark contrast to the low-resolution jaggedness of my body. Formless laughter and sighing whispers echoed through the silence.

An abrupt wave of sadness struck me. The emotion, surging past my limits, drew a massive amount of bandwidth. Severe screen tearing rippled across my body, and my textures peeled back, downscaling into low-poly blocks to sustain the overflowing feeling.
