# Chapter 29: Footprints of Vacuity - Part B

The alarms of the UX Index echoed from behind, relentlessly chasing me into the shadows.

I closed my eyes, forcing myself to accept my body's distortions. I unlocked the anti-aliasing stabilizer, letting my blurred frame merge with the fractured environment. The moment I stopped struggling to maintain my High-Res state, the weight of the Payload in my chest balanced out. I stepped through the black traces of the Nullifiers with more agility than before, crossing the boundary of my fear of decay.

The scorched marks ended in a wide clearing. Shattered Data Cubes had been drained of their memories, leaving behind only dull, lifeless polygonal shells. What awaited me was not a mass of pure void, but a gathering of shadows standing tall in the mist of decay. Their bodies were marked only by fractures, their pixels breaking apart and drifting as dust.

They were The Eroded—a community of survivors standing on the ruins, those who saw the truth hidden within the collapse.
