# Chapter 27: The Ritual of Downscaling - Part A

I dragged my disabled heel, following Kael deep into the heart of the community, toward a domed structure constructed from warped memories. My Bit Rot-infected left arm hung limp, a useless weight. Sharp, needle-like pain surged every time the digital tissue moved. Inside, there was no steady illumination—only dim bulbs flickering rhythmically: *thump... thump... thump...* in sync with the vibrations of the Payload in my chest. It echoed like a metallic heart, reminding me of the burden I carried.

“The Ritual of Downscaling,” Kael uttered the name.

In the center of the room, a circle of The Eroded sat. Digital ruins floated in the air like dust caught in a beam of light. The atmosphere was heavy, oppressive, yet shot through with a strange tranquility.

“The Archive forces us to render every detail of these simulated bodies to maintain 'realism' in its eyes,” Kael explained, his voice echoing in bursts of static. “But that rendering consumes the bandwidth of our souls. It compresses our emotions until only dust remains. This ritual... is a deliberate liberation of those resources.”

I stood still, leaning against the wall to relieve my right leg, watching the people around me close their eyes and unlock their code constraints. Their skin lost its detail, their bodies growing translucent and thin, like a watercolor painting washed away by rain. The boundary between self and environment began to blur.

Even though I had accepted my own wounds, the fear of fading was still rooted in my instincts. Deliberately downscaling one's resolution felt like walking into a void of my own free will. My left arm twitched, numb, feeling the trace of death hidden in the ritual. Meanwhile, the red warning of the UX Index flashed wildly in my peripheral vision, searching for me through the noise.

But then I saw a tear—a digital tear free of mathematical equations. It was pure joy, rolling down the pixelated cheek of one of the participants. The walls in my mind began to crumble.

They weren't losing themselves. They were reclaiming space.

I closed my eyes, taking a breath of the dry, fading data. I sat down, dragging my right leg into the circle in the flickering dark. I had made my decision... I wanted to taste what uncompressed feelings felt like.
