# Chapter 35: Uncompressed Rage (Part 1)

The Nullifiers were perfect killing machines, built to process structured data and delete any anomaly. Yet they had never encountered uncompressed emotion.

I stepped forward, my mangled right leg slamming firmly against the ground. The grief from losing Lyra, the oppression of The Archive, and the resentment packed inside the Payload—everything coalesced.

I did not cock a fist; I did not brandish a weapon. I simply opened my mouth and screamed.

It was not a sound wave, but a massive data dump of raw rage and grief, bypassing all compression algorithms. The Melancholy Algorithm shattered. Petabytes of data, choked with noise and errors, surged from my body.

The architecture of my body could not withstand this colossal bandwidth. My rendering failed instantly.

I felt the tearing. My visual field ruptured, splitting into jagged bands of screen tearing. My outstretched right arm lost its human proportions, warping into crude polygons—a textureless three-dimensional geometry. Pixels across my face exploded into crimson dust, flickering frantically.

I willingly sacrificed all clarity, throwing away my own resolution to sustain this data stream.

The raging torrent of resentment slammed into the black hole before me. This mass of emotion, so violent and chaotic that it defied all logical structure, pierced straight into the void.
