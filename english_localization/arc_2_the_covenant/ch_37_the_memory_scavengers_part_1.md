# Chapter 37: The Memory Scavengers (Part 1)

The fall ended without a sound. Impact — hard surface — slick with something viscous, like rancid machine oil seeping up from below. My right heel-less foot slammed into the ground and shattered at the pixel-level, bursting into a brief scatter of fractured light. I crawled through the Transition Zone on hands and knees, digital fog pressing against my skin like melted plastic, clammy and thick.

I tried to use my left arm to steady myself upright. The arm's tissue was lead-heavy, sluggish with Bit Rot corruption — dense with decay, barely responding. My right elbow ended up bearing most of the load. My neural framerate had dropped to single digits. I felt the Latency in every motion, every pixel of my right foot scraping against the concrete like a wet stone.

A ring of white light cut through the ash-colored fog. Not the cold red of a UX Index scanner — this was halogen-white, hard and blinding, flooding my visual sensors until they flared and blurred.

*"Found one... fresh content."*

A voice corroded by static, metal scraping inside a metal throat, warped beyond any recognizable frequency.

Several silhouettes materialized within the fog wall. Memory Scavengers. Their forms had long since abandoned any pretense of humanity. Geometric metal limbs — angular, misshapen — had replaced natural arms and legs.

On their backs: rigs that looked like iron spiders, studded with dozens of tiny extraction needles each tipped with a pale blue light — the color of data transfer. My instincts told me immediately what they were. Parasites who hunted high-resolution memories, then sold them in the black market.

*"Look at that Texture—still dense. Should fetch a good price if we harvest it,"* one of them laughed. The static-laced sound wave scraped against my simulated eardrums, forced my face into a grimace.

Multiple spotlights converged on me, pinning my frame. They tightened their circle. I dragged my leg backward and felt my spine connect with a wall of rotting data-blocks — no exit. My left arm, heavy and slow, tried to lift itself to shield my chest cavity — the location of the Core Essence and the Payload key.

*"Come on now. Let us copy the best parts quietly,"* a bladed metal appendage leveled straight at me. *"We'll leave your stupid husk behind. Plenty enough left to shuffle around on."*
