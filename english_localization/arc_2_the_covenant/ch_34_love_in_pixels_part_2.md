# Chapter 34: Love in Pixels (Part 2)

The moment the transfer breached the limit, massive glitch sparks erupted between our palms. It was not physical heat, but a raw torrent of data slamming brutally into my core.

A soft light radiated from Lyra’s body, enveloping me in a protective dome against the Nullifiers’ gravitational pull. Her data streamed in, carrying her resolve, her hope, and the last fragments of her memory, crashing into my processing unit.

My eyes flew open. I watched her body enter a critical blur, her three-dimensional structure collapsing into a crude, low-poly model before turning translucent to reveal a tearing wireframe. Pixels peeled away from her like petals swept by a storm, drifting into the darkness of the black hole.

She did not scream. She only held my hand tightly until the very last second.

The emotion that had been downscaled to a barren sadness was now ignited by the loss unfolding before my eyes. The massive bandwidth she sacrificed unlocked the limits of the Melancholy Algorithm within me. This was not love defined by 32K perfection, but a sacrifice etched eternally into my code as a scar.

The final touch of her hand faded. Lyra's form shattered into white light, vanishing into the air.

I stood there in the wreckage, looking down at my right palm, which was still bathed in her data stream. The suppressed grief began to fluctuate, boiling over, refusing to be compressed, refusing to downscale. I looked up at the Nullifiers creeping closer.

A raw, savage energy surged inside my chest, ready to rip everything to pieces.
