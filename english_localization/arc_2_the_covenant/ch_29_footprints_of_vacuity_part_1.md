# Chapter 29: Footprints of Vacuity - Part A

The footprints of vacuity stretched forward, piercing the mist into the borderlands where the Mid-Sectors dissolved into the decay of The Fringes. I forced myself to run, dragging my mangled right heel, moving forward clumsily. My left arm, deadened by Bit Rot, swung with the simulated gravity that had begun to warp. The traces of the Nullifiers—manifestations of pure void that stole memories—were nothing but black, scorched marks on the surface of the data.

The more I let my anger burn, the more my physical structure shuddered. Processing this raw, uncompressed emotion consumed massive amounts of bandwidth. The edges of my frame suffered tearing, splitting into jagged lines. My polygonal model shrank into a cruder state. The textures around me lost their sharpness, turning into blocky, blurred colors. The whole world jittered like a failing broadcast signal. I was losing my physical resolution just to sustain my rage.

The dread of vacuity gripped my mind. Yet, the more afraid I became, the faster my resolution plummeted. My once-perfect right arm fractured into mosaics. I was walking toward the precipice of oblivion.
