# Chapter 32: The Blurred Reflection (Part 1)

I limped closer, balancing the weight of the Payload in my chest to keep from falling forward. Fear spiked my processing rate into overdrive, causing my body to downscale its details once again to make room for the panic.

They were the bruises of a faulty compression process—a digital mold consuming my humanity. An appearance like this was a prime target for the UX Index.

I widened my eyes, staring at the eyes in the mirror. They were now merely flat black dots, devoid of depth. My simulated muscles jittered as I forced a smile, producing a twisted, sickening grin.
