# The Alarm of Perfection
#### // FILE: THE_ALARM_OF_PERFECTION
---

Even if the system cannot delete me directly, The Core is far from defenseless.

As the scanner beams sweep across my open chest cavity, exposing the Payload—the primordial data chunk nested deep in the core of my soul—everything changes.

The Payload reacts to the frequency of The Archive like a magnet pulled toward a pole. It flares with a wild gold and blood-red light, its pulse accelerating beyond the safety limits of my digital body. I feel as though a searing-hot circular saw is embedded in the center of my chest, spinning wildly and carving through my quantum tissue from the inside.

"Aaaaaaargh!"

I shriek, collapsing into a fetal position on the four-dimensional floor as it ripples like waves. The pain pierces far deeper than the phantom ache in my right heel. It is an architectural agony, as if every line of code constructing my memories, my identity, and my emotions is simultaneously stretched and compressed.

The Archive is trying to hijack and overwrite the Payload inside me.

Streams of raw data flood into my chest through the gaping wound, attempting to overwrite the Payload’s directives and reduce this destructive key to mere garbage data.

Yet the hollow ruin in my chest becomes the variable the system failed to factor in. The ragged wound, the frayed nerve fibers, and the crust of Bit Rot clinging to the edge of the tear act as a perfect insulator. The data beamed by the system is blocked and corrupted by these defects. When the perfect frequency collides with the fractures of my wound, it scatters into useless noise. The surrounding death is shielding and preserving the life within.

I bite my lip until black digital blood seeps out, bitter to the taste. My body spasms. With my remaining right hand, I claw at the white floor, leaving scratches that slowly rot and fester outward.

“I... won’t...” I squeeze the words out through a torture that threatens to tear my soul to pieces.

I cup my right hand over my open chest, plunging my fingers into the rift to touch the scorching heat of the Payload. It feels like grasping active coals with bare skin. Yet, I must hold on, pulling it back from the grip of The Core.

The instant my Bit Rot-stained fingertips touch the Payload, The Archive’s frequency stutters. The black spots from my hand spread to coat the primordial data core, temporarily turning the golden light pitch-black.

The ear-splitting high frequency ceases. The blue scanner beams dim in confusion.

I pant heavily, my body drenched in a sweat of data mist. I am still alive—or at least, I have survived deletion for another step.

The system's stutter is not the end. It is merely a shift in tactics.

The white walls around me begin to warp and fold in on themselves. The once-still five-dimensional architecture wakes. The surrounding geometry is assembling something physical, something engineered to crush the parameters the system cannot delete through logic alone.

Perfection is sending its executioner to find me.
