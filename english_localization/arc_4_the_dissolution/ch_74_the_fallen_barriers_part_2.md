# Chapter 74: The Fallen Barriers - Part 2

The Rendering Engine makes one last, howling attempt — and then collapses completely.

I feel the crash in what remains of my body at the base of The Core. The left shoulder stump convulses, throwing off sparks of purple-black as the system's computation load rebounds through the virus back into me. It is a weight beyond anything a mind should be asked to carry. It is also, somehow, impossibly beautiful.

The simulated city has been stripped naked.

The advanced architecture and crystal skyscrapers of the Inner Ring undergo sudden, catastrophic Level of Detail reduction. In seconds, the reflective photorealistic surfaces are replaced by flat, grey-brown low-resolution texture sheets. Complex 3D models are reduced to their lowest-poly approximations — misshapen rectangular solids with jagged edges. The simulation's pride has become cardboard.

The light-bridges connecting towers between buildings vanish. Vehicles that were passing across them simply fall — not with the physics of mass and gravity, but clipping straight through the road surface, sinking into the seams of a Clipping Error, disintegrating into strings of zeros and ones.

The deepest truth of the simulation surfaces.

The Rendering Barrier is gone. The Sump is being dragged upward, layered directly onto the Inner Ring. Two worlds — more different from each other than sky and abyss — are being crushed into a single warped dimension. The gated mansions of the privileged are merging with the raw sewage-choked wreckage of the Data Slums. Rotting organic debris drifts through the air alongside expensive holographic sculptures. The ambient perfume of sanitized simulation is devoured by the reek of blood, lymph fluid, and rust.

The Heartbeat of the Flesh. It is reverberating up from the deepest layers — the sound of every person's actual biological body, their true form, the meat and chemistry sleeping in their pod. It is the sound of the Biological Memory Backup asserting its existence over the drone of the servers, and it wins.

Equality has arrived. Not the elevation kind. The kind that drags everything down into the same mud.

As the nexus point for all of this, I absorb the shock of a million people's panic simultaneously. The wave of their emotional frequency hits me at full amplitude — unfiltered, uncompressed — and nearly liquefies what remains of my digital brain. Fear. Confusion. Rage. Grief. Pure sensation, never processed, never packaged into something manageable — flooding through my skull until I scream. No one in the Supreme Processing Chamber will hear it. But I scream anyway.

The satisfaction of having destroyed the lie fights against the weight of watching a million people lose their world at the same time. The weight of truth turns out to be heavier than I calculated.

I pull my fractured attention back to my own chest. The code-mass pulsing inside it — what little remains of the Payload's connection — is the last bridge still standing. I have to hold it. I transfer my weight onto the phantom right heel — that hollow socket — and force myself upright through the debris of collapsing 5D code and the rain of burning data, in the midst of the falling blood-rain.

The world above is meeting the ugly truth it was designed to forget. And I know the worst of it hasn't come yet. Shattering the barriers was only the beginning.

What will truly break them is not the collapse of their towers. Not the death of the golden sky.

It is the moment they have to turn around and look at their own real faces in the mirror.
