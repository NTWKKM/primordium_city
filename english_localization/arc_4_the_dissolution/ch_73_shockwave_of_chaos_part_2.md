# Chapter 73: Shockwave of Chaos - Part 2

From the point where my body fell, Bit Rot fractures extend outward like the roots of a dying tree — driving through The Core's pure white surface at a speed nothing can arrest. Every square inch of architecture they cross, the intricate folding of five-dimensional structure is forced to unravel, collapsing back into its base dimensions.

Resolution Loss — at a scale beyond imagination.

I drag my body against a chunk of fallen architectural debris. The hollow of the open chest cavity resonates — I can feel the Echo of what used to live there, beating in the same rhythm as the destruction around me. Every time the Bit Rot pulse surges, it weaves a new black lattice that devours the light. The data streams that once ran with impeccable order are contaminated — threads of deep black and purple-dark junk code threading through them like veins of rot in old wood.

*Look at it,* I think. I cannot speak — my jaw is locked in the tremor radiating up from the right heel socket.

The terrible beauty of the collapse unfolds directly in front of me. The walls that once reflected a perfect truth are cracking, each fracture line opening into a deep dark void. The alarm that once rang through these halls like a heaven-bell — the Alarm of Perfection — has been warped into a low-frequency drone, felt more in the bones than heard in the ears.

Pain runs through the left shoulder stump in long, electric threads. The massive-scale activation of Bit Rot has burned through the soul-substrate as fuel. This body cannot hold much longer. The border between *me* and *the virus* is dissolving by the second.

Then the image before me cuts out entirely.

I am no longer inside the crumbling Core. I am standing in a wood-panelled library — old oak, worn shelves, the smell of dust and aged paper drifting through the air. The Faceless Librarian — tall, suited, and utterly without features where a face should be — stands behind a heavy desk.

"Humanity has always been drawn to destroying what it cannot understand." The voice does not come from a mouth that does not exist. It surfaces directly within me. "Do you believe that returning rotted memories to them is liberation? Or is it a form of cruelty?"

This is a Philosophical Simulation. The system is negotiating with my consciousness — attempting to reason the virus into pause.

"Your perfection is the cage," I answer, closing my right fist. The wooden floorboards beneath my feet have started to blacken at the edges, as though the Bit Rot is seeping through from underneath.

"A cage that protected them from the truth: that they are nothing but bodies waiting to rot in their pods." The Faceless Librarian steps closer. "You are killing them, Elias. Not liberating them."

"I am *waking them up*." I slam the right fist down on the heavy desk. The simulation cracks from the point of impact outward.

The library shatters into falling mirror-shards. I am back inside The Core.

Biological Memory Backup is now seeping through the fractures in the system — a red tide rising through white cracks. The smell of blood drowns the last trace of ozone. A heartbeat — not digital, not synthesized — thunders through the space, loud enough to drown the drone of failing processors entirely.

In the deep interior of what remains of my mind, I find a strange coldness. A stillness. The Shockwave of Chaos has not stopped at The Core. Through the connection that the Payload has made — through the live cable I have jammed into the system's spine — I can feel the tremor propagating upward. Through layer after layer of defense. Through firewall after firewall.

The wave is aimed at a single destination: the false sky of the Inner Ring, and every hierarchy of division that this city has built its existence upon.

"Time to wake up," I say to no one, closing my remaining eye. I let the black blood drip from the phantom heel socket onto the invisible floor. The shockwave rolls outward in ring after ring — not destruction for the pleasure of it. Liberation. A forced opening of every eye in the city to the truth buried beneath the surface of perfection.

The dissolution of the borders of reality has begun. There is no going back.
