# Chapter 74: The Fallen Barriers - Part 1

My vision is ripped from the ruined body beneath The Core.

Not Astral projection in any philosophical sense. My consciousness is being injected — distributed — into every cable, every server, every square inch of architecture in Primordium City. I have become part of the detonating Payload. I am the virus. The virus is me.

The pain does not vanish. It is scaled upward. Macro-scale. The phantom ache of the right heel is now the trembling of the city's own foundation. The open chest cavity is now the vast fissure that is tearing the sky of the Inner Ring in half.

I can see the entire city at once, from above — from the perspective of a dying Archive.

For its entire existence, Primordium City has been partitioned by the Resolution Barriers. The Inner Ring: hallowed ground. A golden sky. Classical architecture without seams or imperfections. Citizens rendered at Pristine resolution — flawless, immaculate. Below it, the Mid-Sectors: a haze of compressed data, mid-grade and dimming. And at the bottom, The Sump: the hell-dimension of coarse polygons and organic rot.

The Shockwave of Chaos is shredding every one of those barriers apart.

I feel the exact moment it reaches the Inner Ring. The golden sky — generated by a high-level Global Illumination algorithm running at computational costs the lower districts could never afford — begins to *stutter*. The white clouds that drift through it freeze mid-motion. Their edges fracture outward into large, square pixel-blocks.

Blood-rain begins to fall from the cracking sky.

The Biological Memory Backup of humanity is leaking into the simulation. The golden sky compresses inward, then collapses — raining down as polygon debris, like stained glass ceilings falling onto the worshippers beneath them.

The central system alarm — broadcasting across the false heaven — is not a standard emergency siren. It is classical music, its bit-rate being crushed in real-time, degrading into bit-crushed distortion that grinds across the auditory sensors like broken machinery. The white marble columns rising around the central plaza develop cracks — not physical fractures, not the way stone breaks. The Texture dissolves. Beneath it: bare Wireframe geometry in black and red, the skeleton of the simulation exposed.

Every parameter of perfection that The Archive has been maintaining — destroyed at once. Golden and blue blocks rain from above, passing through rooftops and through bodies. The city's Source Code is unraveling like fabric caught on a hook — one thread pulled, then another, then the entire pattern coming apart.

In this merged perspective, I can feel the breath catch in a million lungs across the Inner Ring. The artificial light goes out. True darkness — darkness packed with static Noise and digital ash — floods in to replace it.

The grey Digital Fog that The Sump has always been confined to rises. It threads through the system's fracture lines, rising upward through the layers. The smell of rotting organic tissue and raw blood fills the places that once knew only the antiseptic scent of a clean simulation.

The barriers that divided the city by class are gone. No preferential rendering for the wealthy. No suppression of resolution for the poor. Every district in the city is being forced to share a single environment.

The environment of ruin. I am the one who brought it to all of them.
