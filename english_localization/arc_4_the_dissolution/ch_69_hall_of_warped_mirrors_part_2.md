# Chapter 69: Hall of Warped Mirrors - Part 2

Three-dimensional combat logic is worthless here. When the first Optimizer drives toward me, it does not travel in a straight line — it erupts from the gap between one blink and the next. The surrounding space compresses, and I feel as though my ribcage is being caved in by a hydraulic press. In the instant the razor edge of its geometry is about to slice through my throat, the raw Payload gaping open in my chest fires.

*THUNK-CRACK.*

Pain detonates from my chest to my brain like a lightning strike through wet circuitry. This is the Logic of Pain — sensation transmuted into survival instinct. The Payload is responding to the Optimizers' frequency and to The Great Purge with violent intensity, scorching through my digital tissue while simultaneously serving as the most precise targeting radar I have ever possessed.

I twist aside by a fraction of a millimeter. The white light-blade scores across my face, leaving a burn that causes a brief Frame Drop in my vision. I don't wait for it to fold dimensions back for another pass. The raw, feral instinct of a biological virus overrides logic.

I hurl the Bit Rot arm — that rotting appendage flickering black and blood-red — forward with everything I have. There is no grace in this attack. It is the act of throwing massed grief and organic filth directly at perfection.

The Bit Rot arm connects with the core of the Optimizer. The sound it makes is not an explosion. It is the sound of Data Tearing — like a cassette tape being ripped apart at full speed. The 5D form shudders and locks up. Black fractures spread outward from the point of impact. Memories of blood-drops and rust corrode its structure from the inside. The Archive has no subroutine to treat wounds made of emotions that do not exist in its database.

In a fraction of a second, the white divine form shatters into coarse grey pixels and rains down as wreckage.

There are still hundreds of them. And the grid-wave of The Great Purge is tightening its radius.

The space begins to expand and contract in violent seizures. The mirror-walls on all sides multiply my reflection into thousands — every one of them being simultaneously torn apart by swarms of Optimizers. I cannot tell which image is my actual position in this dimension. The only thing anchoring me to the present moment is pain.

I use my intact left foot as a pivot, hurling my ruined body through wide, reckless arcs. The asymmetric weight distribution violates The Core's physics engine, generating torque and timing that no algorithm can predict in advance. I spin, I kick, I rake, I tear — destroying the white geometric forms one after another.

Black digital blood sprays from the seam where the Bit Rot arm meets my shoulder and chest cavity. Every drop that falls on the pristine white floor dissolves the architecture around it, leaving stains that reek of copper and rot — and refuse to be cleansed by The Great Purge.

The Bit Rot structure is eating deeper into my left shoulder socket with each passing second. Hallucinations infiltrate the nervous system. I begin to see the Optimizers' faces as the faces of Inner Ring citizens who used to sneer at me. Former colleagues who consigned me to The Sump without a backward glance.

"Surrender... Elias..." Their voices reverberate through the mirrored hall. "Your existence is a bug... Return to... disorder..."

"I'm the one who's *real*!" I roar, voice cracking and layering into Distorted Audio. I lunge forward, abandon evasion entirely, and use my own collapsing body as bait — letting the light-blades slice through my ribs and thigh. If they come to me, I can reach them.

When three of the divine forms charge simultaneously from overlapping dimensional planes, I slam the Bit Rot arm down onto the floor with every ounce of remaining force. The impact generates a Shockwave of Decay — packed dense with memories of storms and death — that detonates outward in all directions, consuming all three attackers and grinding them to powder.

I stand there, heaving. The space around me is carpeted in pixel shards and severed code. The Hall of Warped Mirrors is beginning to fail — its 4D architecture unable to withstand the biological contamination I have unleashed within it.

The path has not opened.

Ahead of me, through the fog of failed rendering and the ozone-glare of The Great Purge, something larger and more complex than anything I have yet faced is taking shape. The architect of the destruction equations. The prime. It has not come to wipe me. It has come to *erase* me — to excise my existence from the historical record of this universe with a permanence that even death cannot offer.
