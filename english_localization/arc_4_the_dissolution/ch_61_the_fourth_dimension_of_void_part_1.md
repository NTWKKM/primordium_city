# Chapter 61: The Fourth Dimension of Void - Part 1

Everything in this place begins with pristine white... a blankness so stark it borders on nausea.

I have just crossed the final threshold, severing the logic of reality from the supreme simulation to enter the very heart of The Core—a domain the human mind was never designed to process. The light surrounding me does not bleed from a synthetic sun or neon tubes; rather, it radiates from the void itself, from every direction, from every shard of symmetrical geometry that constructs this dimension. The light here casts no shadow. And without shadow, depth itself dissolves. I am sinking into a limitless sheet of white paper, the entire world ironed flat until it chokes me.

"Ugh..." I grit my teeth, a low groan escaping through my trembling jaw.

The weight of reality presses down upon my shattered form. The missing heel of my right foot flares with hot quantum needles of phantom pain that race up my spinal cord, pinning me to an agony the system cannot compute.

I drag my right leg across the seamless chrome floor, the rasp of broken data echoing through the silence vacuum. Every step leaves a dark stain of Bit Rot on a surface as pristine white as the bones of a god. The pitch-black smudges spread like drops of ink in a glass of water, sinking deep into the arrogant architecture of The Archive.

Above, my shattered chest lies open, exposing the Payload pulsating amidst a tangled web of fiber-optic vessels. It glows with a dim gold and blood-red light, raw data hemorrhaging to merge with the internal architecture. I feel a draft of pure logic blowing through my chest cavity, freezing me down to my digital marrow, while the charred stump of my left arm still spits static electricity. It is a constant reminder of my violent asymmetry. The body that was once symmetrical has become nothing more than a walking ruin.

I look up at what lies ahead—or perhaps above, or behind. Direction has lost all meaning in this dimension. The space before me folds into itself like paper, yet without leaving a crease. I see my own back walking on the ceiling, which has become the floor on another plane. Stairs that ascend lead down to the deepest pits. Euclidean logic is warped into a grim joke; my three-dimensional self screams in protest against this fourth—or perhaps fifth—dimension, layered until my mind feels as if it will burst.

Every time I blink, the structure of the hall reshapes itself. Chrome pillars twist into infinite spirals; spheres morph into cubes in the corner of my eye. It is a terrifying beauty—a perfection calculated to crush the consciousness of any entity possessing free will.

"You are trying to drive me mad..." I whisper. The words are swallowed immediately by the silence vacuum, casting no echo.

I inhale a breath that contains no oxygen—only pure data mist rushing into my torn lungs. I am a walking glitch in a land of perfection, and I intend to spread this sepsis as deep as my legs... no, my leg and a half, can carry me.
