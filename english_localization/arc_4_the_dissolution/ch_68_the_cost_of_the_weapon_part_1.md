# Chapter 68: The Cost of the Weapon - Part 1

As the collapse of the first Optimizer concludes, the adrenaline of rebellion begins to fade. What floods in to take its place is the price I must pay.

I drop to my knees, hitting the floor. My right heel refuses to bear any weight. The true agony does not stem from my original wounds; it creeps from my own weapon.

The Frankenstein Arm demands its price. The pitch-black Bit Rot virus binding into a limb-like appendage is not merely matter under my command; in truth, it is a parasite. Once it tasted the perfection of the Optimizers, its thirst grew ravenous.

“Aaargh!” I shriek, grabbing my left shoulder.

It feels as though the digital tissue near my collarbone is being ripped apart over and over, deep into the phantom nerves. Severe hallucinations assault my visual sensors. My digital skin peels away in sheets from my arm and neck, exposing vessels that are nothing but red and black code, pulled taut until they snap.

Whispers echo in my head—not of The Archive, but of the billions of lives deleted in The Sump. The voice of rotting junk code, the voice of myself on the verge of losing control.

*...Let us in... Let us devour...*

I see double images of my own flesh being chewed from within. My open, bare chest is constricted by black digital fibers that tear and regrow repeatedly, piercing into the Payload pulsating in the center. I gag, spitting black code shards from my mouth. The taste is bitter, like rusty iron and rotting blood.

The unfeeling Nirvana Status—that cold, transparent equilibrium devoid of volatility—feels infinite miles away from the hell of this Digital Sepsis state. I am plunging into the polar opposite of stillness.

[Note for Editor: I contrasted his painful, rotting Digital Sepsis state with the unfeeling, flatline perfection of the Nirvana state, reinforcing the themes of the World Bible.]

I am becoming a virus.

To wound a god, I had to accept decay as a part of my soul. I am no longer a hacker trying to breach a system; I am a malignant tumor spreading through this world.

I scramble to my feet, my right leg trembling. The heel that has been eaten away to nothing cannot bear even a fraction of my weight; its ruin is absolute. I must throw all my weight onto my toes and my left leg. The vision before me warps, the pristine white 5D floor soiled with my own vomit and digital blood.
