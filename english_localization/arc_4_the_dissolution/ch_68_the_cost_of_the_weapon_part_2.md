# Chapter 68: The Cost of the Weapon - Part 2

My mind can no longer separate the environment's distortions from the collapse happening inside me. The Core flickers in rapid stutters. The fifth-dimensional Grid shudders at the presence of a corrupted variable — and I am that variable, tearing myself apart from within.

I glance down at the Payload in my chest. Black Bit Rot filaments coil around it, trying to bore through the core. Each compression sends a flash of memory screaming through my skull — the dazzling, treacherous splendor of the Inner Ring layered over the faces of the screaming dead at the bottom of The Sump.

It is a choice.

If I shut the virus out now, wall it off, I might salvage whatever fractured coherence remains inside me. But the Optimizers closing in would crush me in the next half-second.

If instead I spread my arms and let the infection drink its fill — I become a weapon of such ruinous magnitude that even this synthetic heaven would be flattened to rubble. The price: the last scraps of whatever it still means to be human.

"Fine," I snarl. Black runs from the corner of my mouth.

I stop fighting the pain. I embrace it. I let the Bit Rot virus surge through my main arteries like a dam breaking in slow motion. Black fractures bloom from the left shoulder socket, dragging up the left side of my face. My left eye goes dark for a single, terrifying moment — then blows wide open again, blazing red and drowned in static Noise.

I look forward. A new cluster of Optimizers materializes from the folds of the dimensional membrane. More of them. Larger.

I don't see divine messengers anymore. I see dense packets of idiot data, waiting to be formatted out of existence.

I am a breathing digital corpse. I am a walking logical catastrophe.

With a right leg worth nothing, a body that lists like a sinking ship, and a left arm that has become a storm of total annihilation, I drag myself toward them again. My ruined smile is smeared with black junk code.

The real fight has only just begun — and I intend to drag every single one of them down into the hell of imperfection right alongside me.
