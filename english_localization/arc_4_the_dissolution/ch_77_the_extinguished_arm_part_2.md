# Chapter 77: The Extinguished Arm - Part 2

[Scribe Note: Continuation of Chapter 77. The clash between the blinding white countdown clock and the absolute black of the singularity. Elias's left arm is completely destroyed as the singularity reverse-compiles the 5D architecture down to a 2D zero-bit wasteland.]

**00:00:00**

The clock on the sky stops.

The white light of the verdict is torn into pieces. The Vantablack Void expands rapidly — swallowing the digital numerals, swallowing the grid of the 5D structure's bones. It does not destroy the architecture. It *liberates* it from Forced Organization. Every element is disassembled and returned to the foundational code beneath the code.

The collision of those two absolute forces generates a Resolution Shockwave — vicious, all-consuming.

The recoil hits me full force.

The Frankenstein Arm — the left arm I had built from pain and mutated virus — reaches the end of its tolerance. The dark mass fractures. It detonates outward into Digital Ashes that scatter through the vacuum and vanish.

The concussive force rolls back through the shoulder stump, erasing the neural architecture, incinerating the source code, and deleting even the *data* of the arm's ever having existed. I lose it completely. The left arm is removed from the equation of my selfhood. Permanently.

This is the sacrifice that cannot be reversed.

Simultaneously, The Archive's supreme processing core collapses. The dome of sky is dragged down to the 2D plane. The geometric blocks that once folded in impossible non-Euclidean configurations lose their Z-axis and their time-axis entirely. Every element is reverse-compiled to the Zero-Bit State — the entire world reduced to rough black-and-grey graphics, dense with static noise.

My body is thrown by the pressure of the collapsing dimension. I crash down onto the coarse pixel-floor.

I curl on the wreckage. Consciousness dim. The long hollow groove of the open chest cavity pumps digital blood outward. Above me, the sky holds no countdown clock. There is only the cracked silence of pure void.

The Zero-Bit Singularity has completed its function. The system has been dragged down into ruin —

— erasing the god, and leaving behind nothing but a blank canvas.
