# Chapter 78: The Ruins of God - Part 2

[Scribe Note: Continuation of Chapter 78. Elias crawls through the 2D ruins, realizing the mission is successful. The Vantablack Void has neutralized the UX Index. He finds peace in the low-poly decay before hearing a voice.]

I use the only hand I have left. I drive the fingertips of my right hand into the flat, block-textured surface of The Core's floor and drag. Both legs are heavy as poured lead. What remains below my right ankle drags and rasps against the pixel ground, scribing a long dark line of ruin behind me.

*Scratch. Scratch.*

The sound of hauling myself echoes through the void. It is coarse and unfiltered — no processing applied to soften it, no score rising beneath it to give it grandeur. Only the sound of a digital creature falling apart and still moving.

Every yard I drag forward, more blood pours from the chest. I cough up shards of broken code — they fall and scatter into pixel-dust that the wind of dying systems carries away.

I am not running. There is nowhere left to run. I only need to reach the edge of the data structure — to reach the place where the horizon of Primordium City was once visible.

When I manage to haul myself up to what remains of a collapsed geometric staircase, I let myself fall against the 2D plane of it. It is cold and hard as a stone inscription.

It worked.

The System Override response has been fully suppressed. The Vantablack Void consumed the countdown clock entirely. The sky is nothing but a grey plate, flickering on and off at irregular intervals. No archangel descends to sort everything back into perfection.

I survived this. The city survived this. Or perhaps we are both dying slowly. The distinction feels academic.

The landscape around me — even riddled with fractured lines, missing surfaces, overlapping Textures that no longer know where they belong — gives me a peace that is genuine. The blinding light that once compelled us all to hide our damage has been destroyed. What is left refuses to be beautiful in any arranged, calculated sense. And that refusal is the most honest thing I have seen in this city.

"So this is it," I breathe. The wheeze from my leaking digital lungs is a thin, high sound. "The Ruins of God."

No UX Index left to assess anyone's Resolution quota. No barrier between the Inner Ring and The Sump. At the rock bottom of this architecture, we have all been pulled to the same plane.

The pain from the absent left arm begins to go numb. The wound at the chest does the same. My cognition is fragmenting — Fragmentation in the literal computational sense, pieces of selfhood detaching and drifting — my identity dissolving into the grey mist by degrees.

I am not afraid. I am not angry.

I simply lean against the remains of a building, and I let my own decay begin to merge with the decay of the world.

The moment I am about to close my eyes and accept whatever comes next —

A frequency cuts through the Static Noise. Not an execution order. Not the voice of any control system. Something faint — a resonance that reflects from every surface in the ruins simultaneously, as if it is emerging from the wreckage itself.

It is trying to speak to me.
