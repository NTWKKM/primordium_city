# Chapter 63: The Prosthetic of Decay - Part 1

The seamless white walls twist and spiral, spitting something forth from their surface. The entity possesses no fixed shape—it is neither beast nor human, but a cluster of five-dimensional geometry moving with absolute precision. Composed of sharp, blade-like angles, it hovers and turns in the air, defiant of gravitational laws.

These are not the Hounds I encountered in The Sump. This is order endowed with intent—entities manufactured to purge physical anomalies.

The geometric shape splits into three segments. Each streaks toward me from impossible vectors: one from directly ahead, the other two emerging from a void the human eye was never wired to process.

I fling myself aside on instinct, bracing my right hand on the floor. Lacking my right heel, I miscalculate my weight distribution and tumble pathetically onto the deck. Frigid blue blades of light slice through the space I occupied a heartbeat ago, leaving smokeless burns on the architecture that the system instantly renders back to perfection.

“Damn it...” I spit, pushing myself back up.

My left arm, severed at the elbow, leaves my balance in ruins. Every time I attempt to ward off a blow or pull myself up, I find myself twitching my left shoulder as if the limb were still there, only to meet empty air and useless ripples of static discharge. My crippled vessel is struggling in a space where the enemy commands the higher dimensions.

The three geometric entities coalesce into a single mass, swelling into a sphere bristling with spikes in every direction. It compresses itself, preparing to unleash a shockwave that will grind me into digital ash.

I retreat until my back slams against a wall that has folded down vertically. I am cornered. My right arm is occupied with supporting my weight and my right leg is on the verge of collapsing at any second. There is no escape. No chance to strike back.

The spiked sphere lunges directly at me. It is far too fast to evade.

In that split second, a feral instinct buried beneath the logical code erupts. If perfection demands my erasure, then I must wield my own ruin as a weapon.

I glare at the stump of my left arm. The pitch-black traces of Bit Rot festering around the wound are boiling, reacting to the fury and agony buried deep within my soul. I stop suppressing it. I open myself up to it completely.

“Come on...” I growl, my eyes locked on the adversary. “Let’s show you what rot tastes like!”

I focus my mind on the void beyond my left elbow, forcing the raw data flowing through my body to surge out and merge with the Bit Rot virus. I draw upon the digital decay that has eaten at me for so long, condensing it, forcing it to take shape.

Black pixel dust erupts in midair, drawing in discarded junk code and system noise from the surroundings like an obsidian cyclone. In the pristine white expanse of The Core, my left arm begins to grow back—but it is no human limb.

It is a prosthetic of decay forged from logical fallacy—grotesque, warped, resembling a charred branch split open to reveal a crimson glow within. It is not bound by bone or sinew; it is connected by the neural fibers of the virus.

The instant its formation completes, a pain far exceeding what a simulated brain can endure explodes in my head.
