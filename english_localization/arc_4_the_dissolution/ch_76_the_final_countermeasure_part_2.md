# Chapter 76: The Final Countermeasure - Part 2

*`[T-MINUS 00:01:30...]`*

The annihilation is accelerating.

I release the pain that lives inside the hollow chest cavity — all of it, unguarded — and let it flood down into the left shoulder stump. I tear open the valve. The most concentrated Bit Rot in my body, coiled at the core, given permission to work. "Come out," I snarl from somewhere deep in the throat, my voice already bleeding into digital noise distortion.

It begins the way it always begins: a sensation of millions of insects erupting from hollow bone. A mass of black and deep-violet pixels surges from the stump — boiling, twisting, cohering fast. The Frankenstein Arm forms from the convergence: viral flesh and garbage code and biological memory residue that has been leaching into the system, fused into something that has no right to exist.

The agony of building this limb is not something a human mind was designed to hold. It is like reaching a bare hand into sulfuric acid while high-voltage current simultaneously rips through every nerve. Every second the arm holds its shape, it consumes *memory* and *cognition* as fuel. I watch a childhood image dissolve — blurred, already faded. A smile whose name I can no longer recall, becoming nothing.

I have no other option.

I plant the enormous Bit Rot arm against the floor and use it as a prop, forcing my body upward. The destructive potential packed into that limb is so concentrated that the luminous white floor surface of the Processing Chamber scorches black on contact. I push. I get my left leg under me — the only leg I have with a heel — and drag the ball of my right foot up from the floor, the stump catching and shrieking across the surface.

I stand.

I face the storm of the Sanitization.

The System Reset's pure white light is immaculate and absolute, sharply opposed to the dark, scarred, biological-blood-soaked viral mass of the arm beside me. Two truths confronting each other.

The dome of atmosphere overhead is being compressed down toward us now, crushing the dimensional space above until only a few meters of headroom remain between us and the ceiling of existence. The sound of my own pulse mingles with the countdown's rhythm.

*`[T-MINUS 00:00:45...]`*

I drag my wrecked body forward, using the Frankenstein Arm as a crutch. It swells as we move, expanding in response to The Archive's light, as if the proximity of absolute order drives it toward absolute disorder — a cornered animal, preparing to tear out the throat of a god.

The Eradication Light lances toward me in a focused beam. I swing the arm up and interpose it. White strikes black — Contrast Snap — the collision so violent that the dimensions around us warp out of shape. A 440Hz pure tone shrieks across every auditory channel. The room's geometry tears open, revealing the empty grid-structure underneath reality — the blank scaffolding of what was, once, a simulation.

"You don't get to delete *anyone*!" I scream. My voice fractures into dozens of layered distortion harmonics at once.

I push through the resistance one inch at a time. The arm is being eaten away by the light, and it feeds itself back by consuming whatever remains of my avatar in a frantic, ravenous loop. I feel the fingertips of my right hand beginning to go translucent. My left leg is losing resolution, its details bleeding away.

A few more meters. Just a few more meters to the system's Main Axis.

*`[T-MINUS 00:00:14... WARNING. CRITICAL CORE OVERHEATING]`*

I wind up. The Frankenstein Arm concentrates its entire viral mass inward, compressing to a point so dark it bends the light around it — a gravitational wound in the simulation's geometry. The 5D dimensional boundary around the arm collapses into a flat, ragged 2D plane. The pain reaches a peak so absolute that I lose my sight for a full second.

There is only the instinct toward destruction to navigate by.

The dimensional ceiling above presses down to the last possible threshold of existence.

And I focus everything — every momentum I have left — preparing to drive this arm of chaos through the heart of The Archive.
