[ CORE.SEPSIS // ARC 04 : CH 63 ]
# The Prosthetic of Decay
#### // FILE: THE_PROSTHETIC_OF_DECAY
---

Aaaaaaargh!

I scream at the top of my lungs, my body convulsing violently. Channeling the Bit Rot prosthetic is no different from swallowing broken glass and acid directly into my nervous system. It feels as if every bone in a left arm that no longer exists is being crushed by iron pliers. At the same time, millions of digital insects burrow and feast upon my soul-tissue from the inside. Welcoming it means letting death flow into every pulse of my logic.

Every movement of those pitch-black fingers sends a searing jolt up to my spine, accompanied by a destructive force beyond measure.

The spiked sphere of perfection is upon me.

I do not dodge.

Roaring, I raise the warped left arm spitting black smoke and recklessly throw my weight forward. The lack of my right heel throws off my balance, and my body crashes into the five-dimensional geometry, the arm built from decay slamming into its outer shell instead of landing a clean strike.

*Crack... Whirrrrr!*

The collision sound is not metal on metal. It is the sound of data rejection. The instant the Bit Rot arm makes contact, The Core's root defense system is triggered. A golden grid of light flares up, barrier-like between myself and the entity. The data wall repels my virus, casting it aside unscathed.

The spiked sphere begins to spin once more, its blue light flashing bright as it prepares to sweep-delete me. To pierce this perfect firewall, my junk-code arm needs more fuel—fuel paid for with my very self.

I grit my teeth until the taste of synthetic blood fills my mouth, forcing myself to tear away deep memory sectors—faded recollections of Earthly breezes, the touch of morning sunlight. I throw them all into the furnace of the Bit Rot as a sacrifice. The prosthetic erupts and swells grotesquely, fueled by the fury of loss. I force my body forward, crashing all my remaining weight into the firewall once more!

This time, the sound that echoes is a violent tear. The virus of decay pierces the golden wall, surging into the pristine structure like a fanged serpent. The five-dimensional form shudders, its blue light flickering rapidly in a desperate attempt to debug the filth. My shattered logic is too volatile for it to compute. Black cracks spiderweb across the entity before it screams in a low-frequency wail, exploding into a shower of useless polygons that soil the pristine floor.

I drop to one knee, panting heavily. The pitch-black left arm slowly dissolves back into a pixel mist, drawn back into the nerve stump at my elbow, leaving only a residue of dark smoke and a deeper burn.

The agony lingers, the smell of burnt ozone and digital blood filling my nose. I look down at my left elbow stump with a mix of dread and self-pity.

I have found my most potent weapon in The Core. Yet this weapon stands ready to destroy me along with my foes.

“If I must become a virus to bring down a god...” I mutter to myself, black sweat dripping onto the geometric surface before me, “then I will rot to the very end.”

I force my broken right leg forward. The wound at my left elbow still shrieks like a ravenous demon. Every step through The Core is no longer just a journey to the center of the system; it has become a descent into my own madness.

Before me, the white architecture begins to alter. It no longer folds into futuristic geometric forms; instead, it starts to mingle with something ancient. Traces of ruins, decaying book spines, and the scent of non-existent dust begin to permeate the atmosphere.

I am drawing closer to the deepest secret of The Archive—the secret hidden behind this manufactured perfection.
