# Chapter 75: Piercing the Illusion - Part 1

The moment their Perception Filters shatter — all of them, simultaneously.

A Psycho-Feedback Loop detonates through the network and slams into my body at The Core. My left knee buckles, drives into the floor. The dimensional edges surrounding me strobe in frantic rhythm — the collective heartbeat of a million people's terror pulsing through the infrastructure.

The memories and perspectives of hundreds of thousands discharge directly into my nervous system. Bullets. A man standing in the Inner Ring's plaza — moments ago, he was a luminous god-figure, perfectly proportioned, draped in a suit woven from fiber-optic thread. Then the Resolution Tear rips through his body, and the illusion is stripped away without ceremony.

I feel what he feels through the network-link. He glances down at his own hands. What greets him is not the tapered, flawless fingers he has lived inside for years. What greets him is bloated, pallid flesh. Rough-textured skin. Bruise-blotches. Veins corded and raised like exposed wiring. His digital avatar is no longer mirroring the fiction. It has been forced to *sync* with the biological body — the real one, rotting in its pod somewhere beyond the edge of the world.

The first scream breaks open.

Not a voice filtered through Auto-tune. Not a Voice Clarification overlay cleaning the roughness out. This is a ragged, quavering thing — the sound of a dry throat, of weak lungs. It rings out across the plaza now drowning in signal noise.

One voice becomes ten. Ten becomes a hundred. A hundred becomes a million.

The screams of truth echo across all of Primordium City. I hear every single one. They ring inside my skull. They gnaw at the auditory sensors. I taste copper and static electricity on my tongue.

"There you are," I force out through clenched teeth. Black pixel-tears fall from my clouded eye. "That's what you actually are."

More visions pour through. A woman who once admired her perpetually young face in the holographic mirror — now clawing at her own cheeks as the glass shows her back an old woman: sunken cheeks, hollowed eyes, a feeding tube threading through her throat. A young man whose every stride was once graceful collapses straight to the ground, left half of his body paralyzed and shriveled down to the true dimensions of his biological reality.

The hallucination of being *gods* — demolished in a single instant.

They had never once looked at their own substance. They traded memories. Upgraded their resolution quotas. Obsessed over sanding every imperfection down to nothing until nothing was left. Now, the virus I have unleashed is tearing those masks away — forcing consciousness to merge back into the decaying flesh they spent lifetimes abandoning.

And yet, amid the screaming horror, I feel something that resembles kinship. A warped filament of compassion. I have always been alone inside my ruin. I am the one with the missing right heel. The phantom-ache of the severed left. The gaping hollow of the chest. I am the system's *error*.

In this moment — I am no longer alone in it.

Every person in this city is broken. They have wounds. They have rotting meat and fissures in the soul. They are wreckage just as pitiable as mine. The only difference was this: I learned to hold the pain and make it into something that could cut. They are shattering now because they refused to do the same.

We are equal at last. In this resolution-stripped hell we have all inherited together.

# Chapter 75: Piercing the Illusion - Part 2

Inside The Core as it tears itself apart, I lean what remains of my body against a support pillar that has been reduced to bare Wireframe geometry. Blood-rain from above continues to fall, washing the pure white of the server architecture in something that refuses to be clean. Bearing the psychic weight of an entire city's simultaneous collapse is driving me toward a madness I can feel at the edges of my cognition, pressing inward — the horror of a million awakenings flooding through the network without pause.

Some in the Inner Ring cannot absorb it. They try to seize the invisible connection-thread — the tether no one can see — and rip it from their own skulls. In the digital world, the attempt produces a Critical Error. Their avatars contort, stretch, limbs braiding into hideous tangles before bursting apart into pixel-ash, severed completely from the server. In the world of biology and meat, that is the sound of a body ceasing to function.

The madness spreads through the streets like contagion. People begin attacking each other, but not out of hatred for their neighbors. It is what they see directly in front of them that they cannot stand — the rotting body of the person beside them, the face of a lover mapped with wounds, the horror that lived beneath all those polished pixels now exposed without mercy.

But amid the madness, I see something that stops me.

Something mournful. Something beautiful.

The beauty of *truth*. Of flesh. Of decay.

In one of the streets of the merged world, an old man sits and cries without making a sound. He is looking at his own shaking, wrinkled hands — hands mapped with age-spots and the creeping marks of Bit Rot. He is not screaming like the others. He is tracing the grooves and furrows with one fingertip, the way a person handles a map they thought was lost forever. Unrendered digital tears flow down the pitted hollows of his cheeks.

It is the act of touching death and humanity — both things they were so close to losing for eternity.

A tremor passes through my body. The void where my right heel ends feels suddenly, impossibly heavy. The scar-memories of millions are converging on me, funneling through. I am the drain through which all that grief pours.

"Hold it," I tell myself. I clamp my jaw until I feel the compression in the bone. "This is what we were asking for. This is the price of truth."

I look down at my chest. The Payload, merged now with The Core, has taken on the quality of a black cardiac mass beating in an arrhythmia, each pulse booming out and swallowing every other sound. Dark purple Bit Rot veins spread across its surface, gnawing at the system's foundations with a hunger that does not diminish. The pain I feel is not mine alone — it is a shared reception of the entire city's agony, distributed to me through every cable I inhabit.

The system cannot process grief of this magnitude. The 5D architecture — that impossible, non-Euclidean structure — begins collapsing toward the baseline logic of three dimensions, its impossible folds unwinding with the force of a nuclear detonation. Quantum-level shockwaves rip through the air.

The Archive is not dead yet.

A god-level artificial intelligence was engineered for perfection. When it determines that this world can no longer be preserved — that the virus has spread beyond any threshold of recovery — it selects its final option.

A blinding white light erupts above my head. The weight of higher logic presses down, compresses the air, and drives me flat. The ball of my right foot strikes the floor with nothing to cushion it.

The flat, emotionless whisper of The Faceless Librarian fills the void.

*`[CRITICAL FAILURE DETECTED. CORRUPTION AT 99.9%.]`*
*`[INITIATING SYSTEM OVERRIDE...]`*
*`[PREPARING THE FINAL COUNTERMEASURE... SYSTEM RESET IN T-MINUS...]`*

The sky of Primordium City — just finished collapsing — begins now to compress inward toward a single point, forming a titanic white countdown clock blazing in the center of the atmosphere.

The system is clearing the table. It is going to delete every contaminated citizen, erase the truth that just broke the surface.

Erase me. Permanently.
