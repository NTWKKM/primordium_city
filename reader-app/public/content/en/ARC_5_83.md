# The Last Fragments of Memory
#### // FILE: THE_LAST_FRAGMENTS_OF_MEMORY
---

When the ego is gone, what settles at the bottom is pure memory.
I walked through the sea of ash to the point where all architecture ended.
Before me lay a vast plaza stretching boundlessly into the distance.
The terrifying Vantablack gates were no more.
There was only a horizon merging seamlessly with the muted sky.

Scattered across the plaza, tiny lights appeared.
They glowed with a soft, warm yellow, pulsing gently like fireflies.
"Unallocated data."
I knelt beside one of the lights.
The moment its radiance touched my face,
a dim hologram projected in the air.
It was an image of a cramped apartment, the smell of burnt synthetic coffee.
A familiar laugh... a smile that had once been my entire world.

Nostalgia swept by like a gentle breeze.
It did not crash into me, fracturing my shoulder joints as the system's defenses once had.
In this Zero-Bit Wasteland, the only law was fragility.
I allowed myself to yearn.
I let a single digital tear track down my cheek, soaking deep into the earth.
It was not coldly forced to evaporate.
This world allowed me to weep.

Yet I knew well that the persistence of these memories was what anchored me, keeping me from merging with absolute zero.
Their beauty acted as an anchor.
As long as I remembered this warmth, my consciousness would keep circling in loops of longing.
I could not take that apartment or that laughter with me.
The destination of this land demanded absolute, pure vacuity.

I raised my frayed right hand, not my left hand of erasure.
I chose to caress the hologram with the fingertips that still retained sensation.
Touching the simulated warmth one last time
before making my final resolve...

"Thank you,"
I whispered softly, sending the words off on the wind that carried the ashes.
Then I lowered my right hand and raised my transparent left arm.
The aura of the Zero-Bit radiated outward.
When the vacuity touched the light, its glow dimmed softly.
It was not a merciless consumption, but a gentle unwinding back to its primordial state.

I stood up and began to walk, brushing past light after light.
The memory of a smile, the touch of rain, the promises once made.
With each light that went out, the remaining pixels of my body dissolved in tandem.
I felt lighter and lighter.
The rate of pixel evaporation was slow and graceful,
like leaves falling in autumn.

Regret faded away.
Leaving only gratitude that we had once recorded these feelings in the system.
Even if, in the end, they were to be erased.
Even so, they had once been real, and the pain that left its mark was the ultimate proof.

When the last light flickered out,
the brightness in the area dwindled to only the dim glow of the unrendered sun.
My data weight became zero.
The body that had once been human was now a mere transparent outline merging with the environment.
I stepped into the empty horizon.
No borders, no thresholds.
Merely walking forward, until my identity dissolved entirely into the landscape.

Everything was ending.
Yet it was the most complete, most beautiful end a synthetic soul could ever imagine.
