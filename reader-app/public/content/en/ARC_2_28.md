# Chapter 28: The Stolen Remembrance - Part A

The first sound that struck my senses wasn't an explosion, but a tearing—like a massive canvas being ripped through by an invisible blade. A shockwave of data slammed into my chest, colliding with the Payload until it vibrated with a heavy, metallic clang like an iron bell. I bolted upright in panic, only to lose my balance and crash to the floor as my missing right heel refused to support my weight.

The resolution around me flickered, dying and returning. The musty scent of compressed data mixed with burnt ozone filled the narrow corridors of the Mid-Sectors.

Cracks split the synthetic concrete floor, leaving behind bubbling pools of pitch-black data like acid corroding the baseline code.

Then the crying began. It wasn't a human sob. It was a sound compressed to the point of losing all its lower frequencies, leaving only an ear-piercing shriek—the scream of a consciousness suddenly stripped of something vital.

“What happened!” I shouted, my voice cracking. My digital body responded to the panic by spitting fractured pixels from my fingertips. My Bit Rot-infected left arm twitched and went numb, so heavy I could barely lift it.

Someone had been robbed. What was taken wasn't physical property; it was memories. A massive memory heist had just erupted in the heart of the community. People scrambled in confusion, some of their bodies flickering like dying neon bulbs. The low-frequency sweep of the UX Index echoed in my head, detecting the chaos and locking onto the 'anomaly' in this sector.

# Chapter 28: The Stolen Remembrance - Part B

I dragged my disabled heel, limping down the distorted corridor. A man lay collapsed against the wall, his body spasming violently, a crack across his head flashing with a red light.

When I reached out with my intact right hand to steady him, a chill shot up my simulated spine. It was the residual shockwave left behind by the thief. The pulse pierced my chest, colliding with the Payload, throwing my data pulse out of sync. In that split second, I felt a piece of my own memory ripped away.

The memory of the first rain touching my skin in the world I had left behind... evaporated. It was completely gone, leaving only a logical void as dark as a black hole.

Anger—a raw, violent emotion I hadn't felt since my feelings were compressed—erupted in my chest, merging with the vibrating weight of the Payload. It was an immeasurable loss. A shard of my identity had been stolen, and though it was a small memory, it was one of the few 'truths' anchor-holding me from the void. All the while, the warning sirens of the UX Index closed in around me.

I looked at the floor. The burning black residue didn't stop there. It dripped, forming a trail of footprints woven from static noise, absorbing the resolution of the surface they stepped on.

I grit my teeth. Letting my anger drive the code in my body, I ignored my ruined heel and ran, dragging my leg after the path of distortion.
