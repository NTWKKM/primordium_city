# Chapter 47: The Parasite in the Chest - Part 1

Grit screamed his warning, his voice compressed and muffled like someone shouting underwater.

I was tumbling into **Digital Psychosis**, the system corruption peaking. The pixels in my eyes toggled between neon green and purple. My vision stuttered violently, the skeletal ruins of The Sump overlapping with the burning, ruined skyscrapers of the physical world. Phantom heat scorched my skin, triggering severe **Artifacting**; dark patches and pixelated distortions crawled from my right fingertips up my forearm.

I wanted to tell Grit to shut up, but my jaw was locked open. Hundreds of terabytes of memory debris flooded into my consciousness.

The taste of acid rain, the touch of warm blood, the wailing alarms of a dying world—everything was packed and rammed into a memory buffer too small to hold it, until the container began to crack.

In that split second, the foreign entity inside me woke up.

The mysterious **Payload**—the malicious code buried deep in the center of my chest that had lain dormant until now—sensed the quantum fluctuations of the **Shatter-Point**.

A meltdown-level heat erupted in the center of my chest. It wasn't just hot; it was expanding.

# Chapter 47: The Parasite in the Chest - Part 2

The agony bypassed all logical limits, translating into sheer physical violence.

The **Payload** in my chest expanded maniacally, squeezing my simulated lungs and heart flat. I collapsed, my knees slamming against the sharp low-poly ground, as I choked up a thick black fluid sludge containing lines of ruined code.

*Crack... Crack!*

The sound of digital ribs being pried open echoed. It felt exactly like a chestburster clawing its way through flesh, aiming for the mirror. The pulsing mass of code sent waves of heat so intense that my chest split, cracking open in blood-red fissures.

"Aaaaargh!" I screamed.

But the sound that tore from my throat was no human language. It was binary screams—a high-pitched electronic screech that sliced into my auditory sensors.

The **Payload** was trying to communicate with the **Shatter-Point**. A surge of dark red data streams shot from my chest, tethering to the cracks in the glass like a parasitic umbilical cord. Old-world code and the parasite in my chest synchronized in a feral interface. My body was nothing but dumb hardware, unable to withstand the trauma of this massive data transfer. My dead left arm twitched violently with each contraction of the Payload.

I was being ripped apart from the inside, my consciousness dissolving into a black hole of data.
