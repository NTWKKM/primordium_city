# Chapter 72: The Torn Connection - Part 1

*AAAAAGGGHH—*

The Core attempts to invoke supreme authority, forcing a 0dB Mute state — sealing all sound frequencies to preserve the equilibrium of its silence. It fails. What erupts from me is not a digital frequency. It is the raw, animal scream of a human being, and it cuts through the silence at the algorithmic level like something alive cutting through something dead. The sound of flesh and will tearing that featureless void apart from the inside. The moment my right-hand fingers drive beneath the Payload mass, the sensation is that of having industrial-grade acid poured directly onto the central processor of my brain.

One strand of raw biological code catches around the fingertip. It attempts to protect itself — writing new code over the surface of my digital skin in real-time. The tissue of my right hand flickers between a bleeding chunk of meat and a shower of dispersed pixels, back and forth, at a rate that defies observation. The smell of the old world's blood overrides the ozone of The Great Purge entirely.

Pulling a code-mass fused with the Root Directory is like extracting a spine through the sternum. Every millimeter I lever it upward, the system inside my body fires thousands of fatal error messages in screaming red directly across my field of vision:

`[FATAL ERROR: BIOLOGICAL CONTAMINATION BREACH]`
`[WARNING: SYSTEM UNRAVELING. ABORT IMMEDIATELY]`
`[ERROR: IDENTITY CORRUPTION AT 89%...]`

I clench my eyes shut, bite through my lip with a force that translates as digital blood flooding from the puncture. My jaw works with a trembling that is not voluntary. The digital blood — black and red, thick as crude oil — wells from the cracks opening in my chest and floats free in The Core Void's weightlessness, hanging in the air like frozen raindrops made of grief.

"Come *out*—"

I gather every remnant of logic, every memory compressed into muscle: the rage at what I lost. The left arm — gone. The right heel — gone. The years rotting in The Sump. The revulsion at a perfection so immaculate it had no room for a single human being. Everything. Compressed. Into the fingers of one right hand.

*RRRRRIP. CRRRK.*

The sound of Base Code tearing reverberates through the Core Void like cathedral bells rung by something that should not be ringing them. My body convulses as though struck by a million volts of direct current. The deep-red data-fibers anchoring the Payload to my digital ribcage snap, one strand at a time. My sense of my own right arm dissolves. My sense of my own legs dissolves. I forget, for a moment, how breathing works.

The only thing remaining in my consciousness is the mass in my hand.

With one final, total, annihilating pull — I rip the Payload free.

*Thwump.*

The chest cavity goes dark instantaneously. The hollow that once pulsed with red light is now simply a void — a death-cavity eaten by blackness. My body has lost its power source and its core logic. The left knee buckles and strikes the invisible floor. The right leg — that architecture from the phantom heel upward — dissolves from the ankle in a wave of coarse pixels that reaches the knee in seconds. Vision fractures like a shattered windshield, the image in front of me splitting into disconnected shards and going dark in pieces. Fractured Vision.

I gulp at air that does not exist. The body is going to dissolve into the void in a matter of seconds.

But in the right hand — the Payload, freed from its housing, detonates into a blazing dark-red oscillation, black and crimson cycling faster than a heartbeat. It hums like a biological storm, craving nothing so much as the destruction of the perfect lie it has been imprisoned in. It is raw. It is savage. And it is packed to the limit with the most truthful, most painful thing I have ever carried: the fact of being human.

"Alright," I whisper. My voice is just radio static now. "You and me. Together."

# Chapter 72: The Torn Connection - Part 2

I force my head back and look up at the Monolith — The Core's pillar of pure white light, standing there unblemished, untouched, and utterly without feeling.

I cannot stand. The right leg, dissolved from heel to knee into coarse pixels, cannot bear weight. I use the Bit Rot-saturated left stump as a brace against the invisible floor, spending the last reserves of structural logic in the body to keep from toppling face-first, and drag myself forward toward the surface of The Core.

Just within reach. Half a meter.

I raise my right hand — the fist still closed around the Payload — as high as the arm will go. The red light of old bloodlines collides with the white radiance of The Core, generating a clash of color that aches to look at directly.

No port to jack into. No channel to interface through. So I have to make one — with the brutality of the Biological Wreckage.

"For the ruin of all of us!"

I drive the fist — Payload and all — into the surface of the Monolith with every last thing I have.

*CRRRRRRRRASH!!!*

The collision does not rebound. The raw biological code-mass *buries* itself through the flawless white surface. Perfection cannot resist a fundamental-level anomaly compressed with this density of entropy. A fracture-line flares across the Monolith's face. The first crack in The Archive's history. The first crack in centuries.

The shockwave of The Torn Connection detonates outward in a ring. It sweeps the crumbling wreck of my body backward — sends it skidding and tumbling. The digital blood and suspended blood-rain hanging in the air are caught by the shock-ring and drawn into the fracture, pulled deep into The Core's wound.

In the fraction of a second before the shockwave finishes, I see the most beautiful thing I have ever seen.

From the single point of impact, Bit Rot fractures bloom outward — dark and total — branching in every direction across the Monolith's pure white surface like a spider web growing at the speed of annihilation. The blazing white light is replaced by blood-red. By the smell of rust. By the grey of static Noise.

The sound The Core once made — a low, perfectly ordered hum — is now a shriek. A cascading chorus of system collapse, uncontrolled and accelerating.

The Archive is infected. The biological-defect virus has taken root in the supreme architecture.

My body hits the invisible floor and consciousness stutters in and out — a light bulb on a dying filament, flashing and dark and flashing. I lie on my back and watch the Monolith fall apart. I watch the collapse of the system that called itself the god of perfection.

The pain in my chest slowly goes still. In its place, a strange quiet settles — nothing like the hollow, engineered peace of the Nirvana Status. This is different. This is the quiet of a man who has accepted his own disintegration completely, who has chosen entropy over pretending, and who finally finds that choice has its own specific gravity.

I close my eyes, and the faintest trace of something that might be a smile crosses my cracked lips. I let the wave of chaos carry whatever remains of me forward — into the new age — into the age of digital ash that is only just now beginning to dawn.
