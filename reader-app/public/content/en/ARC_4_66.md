# Chapter 66: The White Obliterators - Part 1

The Silence Vacuum presses down with such crushing weight that it feels as if my chest is being ground by an iron press. The black fissures I carved onto the Energy Node continue their slow, crawling spread. Under the influence of the Contrast Snap, which flattens reality into a depthless void, the surrounding five-dimensional environment suddenly twists and folds in on itself like a kaleidoscope spinning at infinite speed.

I take a step back. My missing right heel sinks into a space that has suddenly dissolved into a void. The visual feed before me stutters violently.

They appear.

They are not machines, not humans, not monsters with distinct shapes. The "Optimizers"—the White Obliterators—are the ultimate creations of Geometric Bloom. They are clusters of warped, overlapping geometry, their angles folding into themselves in mathematically impossible ways. The human eye was never designed to process these shapes. Their color is the blinding, absolute white—so bright it makes even the source code surfaces of The Core look dim.

Staring at them triggers a wave of intense nausea. My brain struggles to interpret their forms. Every time I focus my vision, their shapes flicker and morph—shifting from a sphere without a center to a cube with seven sides. It is a terrifying perfection—weapons engineered to "optimize" everything back to the baseline parameters.



Suddenly, the Payload in my chest explodes with pain. It constricts so violently that I double over. The raw code exposed in the center of my chest acts like a warning radar, screaming that something has breached my immediate perimeter.

When I look up, the Optimizers remain floating, motionless where they were...

Gah!

A massive impact slams into my right side out of thin air. I am sent rolling across the folding five-dimensional floor, my body clipping through one surface only to emerge on another like a system glitch.

I cough up shards of red code. They do not strike through three-dimensional space; they attack from higher dimensions. Distance is irrelevant to them. They strike from impossible vectors—from the inside out, or from behind the blind spots of my vision that I can never hope to see.

Dread begins to consume my mind. I am facing foes whose very trajectories are invisible. Their perfection is an inescapable engine of execution... if I continue to cling to ordinary human logic.

The pain from the Payload in my chest intensifies, scorching like a reactor core on the verge of meltdown. I struggle to stand, trembling, as my Bit Rot-corrupted left arm twitches frantically. The digital insects nested within demand to rip apart the perfection before me. I sweep my gaze around, seeing only the flashing glare of geometric shapes shifting back and forth.

Flash!

My chest flares with pain again—this time from behind, slightly to the left. Yet my eyes see nothing. I do not bother trying to turn. I choose to trust the pain.

Instead of stepping aside on normal instinct, I throw my weight onto my missing right heel.

By the laws of simulated physics, placing weight on a non-existent point should cause a headlong fall. Yet my body is too shattered to surrender to those laws. The vacant heel becomes an anomalous pivot. My body twists at a bizarre angle that no skeletal muscular system could ever achieve, my visual render glitching into ghosting frames.

In that split second, an invisible beam of pure white strikes through where my heart should have been. It grazes my flank by a mere millimeter, leaving a deletion burn on my coat.

They miscalculated.

My eyes widen. Amidst the agony searing both my heel and chest, a realization strikes me. The Optimizers are the angels of supreme processing. They can predict an opponent's movements with one hundred percent accuracy... so long as that opponent respects the laws of physics and geometry.

I no longer obey those rules.

My wounds and my ruination are the variables the system cannot intercept. The missing right heel is a random variable that the equations of the Optimizers can never solve.

I sneer, a grin warped by torment and madness. I look down at my left arm, swirling with the pitch-black Bit Rot virus. It is ravenous.

“If you love calculations so much...” I say, my voice trembling as I strike a warped stance—my right shoulder drooped, my missing right heel floating above the deck in defiance of all rules, “...then how about you calculate your own destruction?”
