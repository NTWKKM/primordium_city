The moment I crossed the threshold, the far edge of The Abyss convulsed. The low-poly geometry surrounding me was torn apart at the seams. Blinding light erupted from above — the UX Index initiating Purge Protocol. A tide of clean white geometry swept forward, consuming The Sump's darkness, converting ruin into void. The smell of synthetic ozone — sharp, chemical, institutional — clashed with the rust and rot of entropy. The collision of two opposing aesthetics warped the air itself; the edges of my vision jerked and stuttered like corrupted frames.

Before me stood the largest logical aberration in this particular hell — a Shatter-Point of monstrous scale. Its surface shimmered like a vast mercury mirror, rippling in slow, hypnotic arcs. And in that surface, something gazed back at me.

Myself.

The reflection was pitiless in its accuracy. The shoulder socket where my left arm had been — a jagged void of failed geometry, a place where existence had simply refused to render. The right heel sheared to nothing, my whole gait a broken, lurching negotiation with gravity. The Payload in my chest cavity bulging grotesquely against the ribcage, the hollow open wound pulsing black code outward in a slow, infected rhythm. Bit Rot crawling across every surface of me like fatal mold — the geometry dissolving inch by inch, eaten by its own corruption. This was no longer a human form. It was digital wreckage waiting for a scheduled deletion.

The eyes in the mirror stared back. Empty, and aching with a suffering no line of code could parse.

The shadow in the mirror moved. The cracked, desiccated lips of my reflection parted, and a whisper rang directly inside my skull — not an echo, but a splinter of my own dissolving thoughts turned against me.

*"Stop..."* The voice trembled with pathetic sincerity. *"You've hurt enough. Look behind you — the Purge Protocol is coming. That light will wash all of it away. Let this body become a Hollow... let the perfection take you. Just close your eyes. The dragging of your broken leg will end. The tearing sensation in your chest will be over."*

The heat-wave of the Purge Protocol pressed at my back. White grid-lines were consuming the wreckage behind me, converting filth into pristine emptiness in smooth, relentless rows. In the mirror, my reflection wore a terrible peace. It offered a way out of hell, and something deep in my processing reached for it — the gravity of wanting to stop, wanting to unclench from the agony of consciousness, wanting to release the last grip on self. If I simply let go of awareness, every torment would dissolve.

The white light said: *we will make you clean.*
