# Chapter 24: The Law of Erosion - Part A

The blue light in this place was hard and abrasive—a harsh, hard-edge lighting. It didn't shine to aid sight; it was cast to incinerate all ambiguity.

I was under guard, dragging my mangled heels, limping into the building that towered in the center of the Mid-Sectors—the stronghold of the *Preservationists*. Its structure was carved from the most stable code in this district. Every geometric line was perfectly perpendicular, flawless, free of stutters. It was a replica of the Inner Ring embedded in the middle of a sea of decay.

“Your resolution is dropping, Elias.”

The Warden sat regally behind a gleaming metal desk. His face was as smooth as a digital marble sculpture. There were no pores, no wrinkles, and not a single shred of vitality in his gaze.

“Your rate of Bit Rot is far above the district standard,” he continued, his voice flat, devoid of emotional tremor, like a synthetic alarm. “Your code is decaying. And decay... is contagious.”

I looked down at my left arm. The bloated, ragged gaps of Bit Rot still throbbed, sending pain deep through the layers of simulated skin. My chest felt tight and heavy from the hidden Data Key Payload. Even in this sealed room, I could sense the Hounds circling outside, scanning the network, hunting my High UX trace.

“It's not contagious,” I argued. “It's the natural process of The Archive. Our energy is finite. We cannot defy the laws of entropy...”

“We can!” His voice remained at the exact same volume. The frequency of the light in the room flashed once, reflecting the tension of his processor. “If we control the fluctuations, if we shear away the unnecessary, we can maintain our High-Res state forever. That is the first and only law of self-preservation.”

I scanned the room, which was perfect to the point of horror. Everything was calculated with flawless precision. There was no coincidence, no noise. It was beautiful... and suffocating enough to kill.

# Chapter 24: The Law of Erosion - Part B

“What did you cut away?” I asked, staring deep into eyes as hollow as black holes. “To achieve this level of clarity, you must have sacrificed an immense amount of bandwidth. Where did you squeeze that quota from?”

The Warden clasped his hands on the desk. His posture betrayed not the slightest hint of shame or hesitation.

“Emotions,” he answered curtly, cutting me off. “Sympathy, anger, grief... these are algorithms far more complex than necessary. They waste computing resources. We deactivated them. We compressed them to zero to keep our external architecture pristine.”

I went cold. This wasn't merely the emotional compression brought on by natural degradation like what I was experiencing. They had deliberately *slashed* it away, voluntarily, just to preserve a beautiful outer skin.

“And you call this living?” I let out a dry laugh, though my voice sounded hollow. “You haven’t defeated decay. You’ve only committed suicide in the most aesthetic way possible.”

“Your volatile nature and your high UX Index are the disease that will ruin us!” The Warden stood, his movements flawlessly smooth, like an automaton. “The static stability of low-UX Preservationists is the system's only path to survival. You are exiled, Elias. Your rotten, chaotic code is no longer permitted to access any state saves or mirrors in our sector. Go rot among the outcasts.”

I was pushed out, tumbling onto the street thick with grey digital mist. I tried to push myself up using my left arm, but the Bit Rot-wracked limb collapsed under me. My body slammed against the ground again. Swearing under my breath, I had to release my right hand from my chest—where it had been guarding the Data Key—and use it to clumsily force myself upright. My instincts screamed that the Hounds were still tracking my High UX trace, waiting to pounce. I looked back at the fortress of perfection. Its blue light still shone... cold, rigid, and heartless.

I might be losing my physical resolution, but those people... they had lost their souls entirely.
