Pain is not a wave. It is an ocean, and it does not stop crushing.

The smell of burnt ozone mixed with digital rust hung in the air. Black raw code seeped from the void at my shoulder like blood that couldn't be stanched.

My vision bled crimson. Error alerts burst across my field of sight in cascading waves, drowning the landscape. Phantom Pain — the ghost agony of fingers I no longer possessed — screamed along nerve-lines that mapped to nothing, chewing through my processing bandwidth and forcing me to confront a loss that no patch could repair. Once, in another life, I would have reached for the suppress-protocol, dissolved the sensation into background static. Today, I let the torture remain. The agony — massive and absolute — was the only evidence left that I hadn't yet hollowed out into one of The Hollows. That I still had something inside worth being destroyed.

To stop moving is to consent to death. I pressed my right arm against the ground and forced myself upward out of the dark.

Balance was a waking nightmare. With the right heel sheared away and the left-right symmetry of my frame destroyed, every time I planted weight, pain detonated from the leg and lanced up through the spine. I dragged the ruin of myself forward like a corpse that had forgotten to stop being stubborn.

But the worst of it wasn't the physical wreckage. It was the Payload lodged in my chest cavity — that hollow open wound in my torso, pulsing black and grotesque. Its rhythm was wrong, arrhythmic, swelling against my simulated ribcage like a metal parasite trying to lever my sternum open from the inside. Every heartbeat of it pressed outward. Every step I took, I felt it shift.

Every stride was an act of theft from a body that was already trying to collapse. I dragged my legs through the logic-cracks, stepped across the boundary between The Sump's black depths and the light bleeding from The Core — leaving a trail of rotting code-drops behind me, carrying the price of survival forward, toward whatever truth **The Archive** had buried at the center of everything.
