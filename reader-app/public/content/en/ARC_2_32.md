# Chapter 32: The Blurred Reflection (Part 1)

In a corner of the library, a large mirror leaned against the wall, its metallic data frame rusting into brown pixels. This glass was a Quantum Interface, reflecting the fundamental state of one's code.

I limped closer, balancing the weight of the Payload in my chest to keep from falling forward. Fear spiked my processing rate into overdrive, causing my body to downscale its details once again to make room for the panic.

The reflection staring back was not me. The clouded glass was saturated with running noise. What stood there was a ruin.

I raised my right hand to touch my face. The feel of it was as rough as sandpaper. The once-fine textures had been downscaled into rigid geometric shapes. My jawline faded, blending into the background.

The most horrifying sight was the distorted streaks of purple and green compression artifacts blooming across my neck, spreading down to my numb left arm. They were the bruises of a faulty compression process—a digital mold consuming my humanity. An appearance like this was a prime target for the UX Index.

I widened my eyes, staring at the eyes in the mirror. They were now merely flat black dots, devoid of depth. My simulated muscles jittered as I forced a smile, producing a twisted, sickening grin.

# Chapter 32: The Blurred Reflection (Part 2)

Anguish struck me silently.

As my emotions plunged to their nadir, drawing bandwidth to process despair caused screen tearing to ripple across my body. My reflection jittered, flashing between a low-resolution texture and a jagged polygon wireframe.

If memories are compressed, if feelings are reduced to mere fragments, and if my face is too distorted to recognize... am I even still Elias?

I stood staring at the glitched mass of data in the mirror, its vacant eyes devoid of life. The fear that should have poured out was compressed into a tight, dull ache in my throat. The weight of the Payload in my chest underscored my own wretchedness. I wanted to shatter the glass with my right fist, but I could only release a sigh like a low-frequency hum.

Bit Rot was eating deep into my core essence.

I pressed my right hand against the glass, matching my reflection. The mirror's surface rippled, sending a chilling cold through my dead pixel scar.

"Are you still there…?" I whispered, my voice pitch-shifted and distorted.

There was no answer, only the crackle of static rising from the compression artifacts on my face. I closed my eyes, surrendering to the reflection, letting the blur swallow the last fragment of truth.
