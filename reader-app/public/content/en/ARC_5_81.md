# The Taste of Absolute Vacuity
#### // FILE: THE_TASTE_OF_ABSOLUTE_VACUITY
---

I opened my eyes once more, with no concept of how much time had drifted by.
The counterfeit perfection was gone, erased.
Primordium City, once so seamless and sterile, now lay in magnificent ruins.
Supporting my broken form, I dragged myself upward and stepped onto the jagged, fractured earth. It was no longer the desert of absolute silence; it was the Zero-Bit Wasteland.
The sky overhead did not burn with a blinding glare. Instead, it was washed in a muted orange-gray, digital ashes drifting down slowly, like the snow of shattered memories.
They brushed against my frayed skin, evaporating without a trace.
The sun hanging above was an unrendered sun—a blocky, low-res pixel cluster radiating a pure, unfiltered warmth. Its light did not glint off glossy surfaces; it only exposed the raw texture of decay.
Every limping stride, leaning my weight onto my left foot to spare my mangled right heel, crushing down on missing data, felt more real than anything I had ever touched.

I stopped.
I looked down at my left arm.
It was transparent.
Void of color, devoid of texture, empty of any circulating code.
0-byte load.
Zero-bit limb.
The arm was a void holding the memory of human form.
Weightless, as if it had never existed... yet it was the only thing anchoring me to this new world.

The air no longer carried the panic of an apocalypse.
It was static peace, the quiet grace of acceptance.
Cessation was not a terrifying end. It was rest.
The taste of this land was not bitter; it was a warm, soft sorrow.
I inhaled a breath composed of shattered code-dust, letting go of the false purpose I had clung to for so long, letting it scatter into the wind.

The survival instinct that once shrieked inside me had quieted to a whisper.
The ground beneath my feet trembled faintly, a slow, rhythmic pulse of a dying world.
Digital ashes continued to fall, settling over the debris of what had once been skyscrapers, leaving only rough-polygon steel skeletons.
The pixelated sun cast an earthen-orange glow across my transparent left arm.

*Crack...*
A soft fracturing sound echoed from the structure ahead.
It did not make me flinch.
The compression artifacting that had once brutally torn at my left shoulder had softened into a gentle shedding.
The pixel blocks at the boundary of my flesh and the void slowly dissolved, free of pain.
Fear was replaced by understanding.
I was not losing myself; I was returning my essence to the ecosystem.

I reached out with my right hand, which still retained a human outline, to catch the falling ash.
The moment it touched my skin, the ash dissolved into gray code, seeping into the fissures of my pixels.
*The world has broken...*
And it was beautiful.
No system of The Archive hunted here to erase anomalies.
Anomalies were the only things that remained.
I walked through the veil of falling ash, letting the wind of vacuity blow through my hollow chest.
The emotions that had once been flattened and suppressed began to stir.
I no longer had to force myself to be a straight line.
I could feel sorrow, I could feel loneliness, and I could feel peace, all at once.

Accepting death was not surrender.
It was allowing oneself to rest in a world that no longer demanded perfection.
My zero-bit arm swung with the rhythm of my steps.
I was the sole survivor in this wasteland.
And I was moving forward, into the core of silence.
