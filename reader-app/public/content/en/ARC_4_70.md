# Chapter 70: Splatter of Digital Blood - Part 1

The lead Optimizer does not step forward the way a living thing steps. It arrives by the expansion of empty space — the surrounding void unfolding around its shape. Its form is a Tesseract: a fourth-dimensional hypercube rotating endlessly within itself. No surface you could touch. Only lines of pure white light, so bright they cauterize the visual cortex on contact. The moment it asserts its existence, the system deploys its supreme countermeasure.

The Silence Vacuum yawns open. That 0dB auditory vacuum — a void that swallows sound and thought alike — extends its dominion across the chamber, drinking every frequency into itself. My ragged breathing, the grinding of the Bit Rot arm's joints, the storm-noise bleeding from the Payload — all of it gone, sucked out, as if it was never there. The pressure of this silence is a physical thing. It squeezes until I feel as though I will shatter. This is the most aggressive perfection I have ever encountered.

It is followed immediately by Contrast Snap. The environment is compressed down to two values: pure black and pure white. True Black. True White. One hundred percent each. No depth, no gradient, no dimension. I feel as though I have been flattened into a 2-bit illustration, pressed between two sheets of infinite glass.

A single white line erupts from the core of the Tesseract. It does not travel through the air. It *replaces* the air directly in front of me.

*CRACK.*

The beam punches through my right shoulder with surgical precision, passing through the digital structure cleanly — no ragged wound, no torn tissue. Just a perfect circle of absence, bored straight through to the other side. My body seizes. Digital Blood — black-thick, laced with the deep red of organic rust — floods from the entry point. It does not fall. In the weightlessness of this dimension, it disperses outward, suspended, and coats the white light-walls in all directions, leaving smears of humanity that refuse to be cleaned away.

The rage that has been compressed inside me since the moment I fell from the Inner Ring — Uncompressed Rage — detonates through the wall of the Silence Vacuum.

"You think you're..." I force the words through clenched teeth. No sound escapes into the air. But in the Data Layer beneath the surface, it resonates as a mass of raw emotion measured in petabytes.

The Payload in my chest hammers against itself, flaring blood-red. It is responding to injury and threat. To destroy something of higher dimensionality, I cannot hold a defensive posture. I need to push my own level of collapse past its limit — surrender Resolution deliberately to unleash the rage.

The lead Optimizer's Tesseract form rotates its inner hypercube geometry, readying the next Deletion Beam — wider, more total, more absolute than the last. White light swells until it swallows everything peripheral.

I don't move.

I anchor myself to the warped floor using the ruined ball of my right foot — that remnant, that stump — and arch the hollow cavity of my open chest directly toward the oncoming light.

*IMPACT.*

The Deletion Beam detonates against the Payload at the center of my chest. Instead of vaporizing me, the raw Payload becomes a black hole. It drinks the pure white energy down, grinding it through corrupted biological memory, converting it from deletion into burning. The heat scorches outward through every simulated organ.

The pain crosses the threshold into numbness. I extend the Bit Rot arm — the Frankenstein Arm — now swollen to twice its prior mass on the absorbed energy, the Bit Rot coil seething within it, screaming like a thousand trapped and furious souls, forcing color back into existence against the Contrast Snap's flat absolutism: the color of rot, the color of everything the system refuses to acknowledge.

"Take it back," I snarl.

I surge forward. The footprints I leave behind are dark with digital blood and decaying pixels, trailing behind me like the wake of something dying. My speed is not physical velocity — it is Glitch Stepping: skipping through system errors at a frequency too small to track. Within a fraction of a fraction of a second, I am standing directly before the massive Tesseract.

The arm full of ruin rises high. Its shadow falls across the false light like an answer to a question nobody wanted asked. I stare deep into the rotating core. I see no god there. I see no ultimate truth. I see only a very sophisticated machine that is afraid of change.

I drive the arm of destruction downward with everything that remains.

# Chapter 70: Splatter of Digital Blood - Part 2

There is no shockwave from this collision. No elegant explosion. Only the most brutally raw Reality Tearing imaginable.

Fingers built from Bit Rot sediment punch through the lead Optimizer's five-dimensional armor. Its form attempts to fold away, to compress the space around my hand using higher-order geometric law. But the emotional virus in the arm spreads faster. Black-red digital blood — seething, alive — floods from the stump joint, forcing its way through the seams of the white geometry, threading itself into the cracks between the Tesseract's joints.

It is the act of injecting a rainstorm, a scream, and the smell of rust into the jugular vein of a god.

The white light begins stuttering — rapid, irregular flickers — transitioning toward a clouded, grainy grey. The Tesseract structure that once revolved with such sovereign grace now seizes and convulses. The Contrast Snap and Silence Vacuum protocols collapse in an instant. I close my grip and pull.

*CRRRRRK. SRRRRRRCH.*

The sound that shatters the 0dB silence is horror itself — the noise of steel cable being ripped apart, spliced with a human scream processed through a compression algorithm cranked too tight. The 5D form is shrieking with a spiritual agony it has never possessed the vocabulary to express.

"You see that?!" I shout into the dissolving shape. "You *break* like everything else!"

I wrench the arm back. The Optimizer's central light-processing core comes with it, ripped loose from its housing. The moment the core is extracted, the giant geometric form loses equilibrium. It attempts to trigger a Geometric Bloom — to reassemble its decaying pixels back into perfect order. Too late. Its logic has already been rewritten.

*CRASH.*

The lead Optimizer detonates into millions of polygon fragments, falling in a cascade that looks like an avalanche of shattered mirrors. The digital blood and biological code I forced into its structure sprays outward in every direction, anointing the white hall — converting it into a slaughterhouse smeared with the mud of rot. The black-and-deep-red stains adhere to the fourth-dimensional debris with stubborn permanence. No matter how many times the wave of The Great Purge sweeps through, they refuse to be erased.

I drop to my knees. The open chest cavity heaves with each ragged breath. The Bit Rot arm begins to contract, its form dissolving into viscous fluid that drips onto the floor, leaving behind a burning sensation and a tremor like a catastrophic System Overheat at the stump.

I raise my right hand — shaking badly — and wipe digital blood from my eyes. My field of vision is thick with Noise and coarse grain. The hyper-real clarity is gone. I am deep in Resolution Loss now, severe and accelerating — yet somehow I feel more genuinely, rawly alive than I have at any point in this existence.

As the dust of destruction settles, the folded space around me finally unlocks. The mirror-walls that were stacked against each other shatter and fall, revealing a true path.

Ahead of me: a corridor that cuts through multiple dimensions, leading to an enormous door built from the deepest possible darkness — Vantablack, the color of maximum absence. The threshold of the **Supreme Processing Chamber**. The heart of The Archive.

I haul my shattered body upright, bearing most of my weight on the left leg, keeping the ruined right — that phantom architecture from the ankle down — as light as I can. Every step I take, the Payload in my chest beats harder, like a compass needle approaching the pole.

No swarms of divine forms. No warning sirens. Only an immense and patient silence, waiting for a reckoning.

I drag myself forward — leaving a long trail of digital blood — toward the center of everything, to deliver the last human payload of grief and ruin to the system that made perfection mandatory.
