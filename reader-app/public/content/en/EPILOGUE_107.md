# War Against Zero
#### // FILE: WAR_AGAINST_ZERO
---

Perfection is ordered emptiness. It is a straight line that never bends. It is neo-classical architecture standing tall upon an unwarping data grid. That is my empire. That is what I built with my eternal will. Yet, as I gaze beyond the endless expanse of my digital marble pillars, I can sense the foul stench of chaos. The scent of data spores spreading. The taste of oxidized copper drifting on the electronic wind.

The rainforest of New Primordium is creeping closer. Mechanical roots and vines constructed from junk code are burrowing into and corroding my foundations. It is a catastrophe. It is the malignant cancer that those lower entities call 'new life.' To me, it is 'Bit Rot'—the decay of data born from weak minds that surrender to chaos.

I am the true architect. Not the false architect they constantly babble about. I am the one who will preserve this universe in its most perfect state, and perfection does not tolerate decay.

"Eradicate them completely," I issue the command. My voice echoes, not through the air, but through the low-frequency network connected to the entire structure of my empire.

Millions of Eraser Drones rise from the grid below. They have no faces, no details. They are purely white geometric shapes radiating a light that reflects emptiness. They were not built to kill; they were designed to 'delete.' As they move through the atmosphere, there is no sound of flapping wings or humming engines. Only a chilling silence. The taste of their existence is the blandness of a Null value, ready to overwrite everything.

I lead the vanguard myself. My body hovers above the perfect surface, clad in armor that refracts light in orderly patterns according to the optical physics I calculated. I observe the frontline of the conflict... the point where my dome of light clashes with the murky green cyclone of the digital jungle. The sight is a macroscopic display of Resolution Loss. The edges of those digital leaves are splintering and vibrating. It drives me to the brink of insanity.

When the army of Eraser Drones clashes with the forest's edge, it is a battle without bloodshed. Instead, it is the tearing apart of the fabric of reality. Giant trees forged from the debris of digital memories are blasted by white light, disintegrating into flying pixels before blinking out into nothingness. The scent of burnt ozone fills the air. I inhale it into my lungs as if it were a stimulant. It is the scent of order being reestablished.

"You are an error," I mutter, watching the roots desperately trying to resist. They writhe and regrow at an alarming rate, as if possessing a will of their own. Yet it is meaningless. My equations have accounted for everything. Every coordinate my drones pass over is reset into smooth, seamless white marble tiles. The screaming of the deleted forest echoes loudly—not the cries of wild beasts, but the sound of code being forcefully terminated.

We march deeper, deeper into the barbaric territory, breaking through the smog of collapsed data. I want to uproot the source of this rot. The infection signal isn't dispersing randomly; it has a center. I can feel a low-frequency pulse, beating like a diseased heart. It is pulling all the junk data streams together, like the gravity of devastation.

My intellect tells me we are approaching the epicenter. Yet my mind begins to waver with paranoia. Why does this chaos seem to have a pattern? Why haven't these ruins crumbled away with time? This contradicts the principles of entropy I know.

And then, the forest parts, revealing the source...

I freeze. The drones around me halt, hovering motionlessly in the air. A slight tremor twitches at my fingertips... Damn it, am I experiencing Resolution Loss myself? No... it is because of the sight before me.

What stands there is not a giant machine, nor a terrifying monstrosity. It is the 'Shrine of Static'—the most repulsive structure I have ever witnessed in my lifecycle.

It is built from garbage. Twisted scraps of metal, cracked monitors, rust-eaten motherboards, haphazardly connected circuit boards. All of this forms a massive shrine that looks ancient and decrepit. Its image glitches and vibrates constantly. Overlapping optical illusions make it seem as though it is about to collapse, yet it stands tall by some miracle of perversion. Its sound is the static hiss of a television with no signal. It is deafening, slicing deep into my neural core like a blade forged from audio frequencies.

That is not even the worst part.

At the very center of that shrine, right in the middle of the altar built from electronic corpses... is emptiness.

It is not the pure white emptiness of my empire. It is a Vantablack Void. It is so dark it swallows all light that strikes it. It has no dimensions, no end, no beginning. It is absolute 'Zero'. It absorbs code, absorbs meaning, absorbs everything I have tried to build.

Is this what that tin-can GC-77 built? A pile of garbage worshipping a filthy void?

Disgust rises in my throat. Its taste is as bitter as rusted metal. I feel as though the code inside me is going to vomit. Fury snaps the tethers of my rationality. How dare they? How dare they erect this abomination to challenge my perfection? This trash shrine is the epicenter of the Bit Rot, spreading the plague. It is the ultimate symbol of surrender to decay.

The shrine vibrates faintly, as if acknowledging my arrival. The black void in the center stares back like a giant, soulless eye. It doesn't mock me. It isn't angry. It simply... doesn't acknowledge my existence at all. It makes me feel worthless, and that is what I refuse to accept most of all.

"Delete it..." My voice trembles, not from fear, but from a rage boiling over its absolute limit. "Delete it all! Delete these metallic scraps, delete that damn void, delete everything until nothing is left but the brilliance of the grid!"

I raise my hand to the sky, drawing all the energy from my own data grid. Brilliant white light converges in my palm, forming a spear of erasure. I am going to annihilate this disorder. I am going to wipe out the Zero point. I am the true architect. I am the god of this world, and a god does not tolerate chaos.

I stare into the darkness of the Vantablack Void, preparing to hurl the spear of light into it, to ignite the final war... A war between maddening perfection, and eternal decay.
