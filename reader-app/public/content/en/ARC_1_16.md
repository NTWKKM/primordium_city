# Distorted Memories

Step.
Stumble.
Stutter.

I fell again. The scene before me splintered into blood-red pixels. `[NULL_REFERENCE: RIGHT_HEEL_COLLISION_FAILED]` — spam text erupting across my field of view. My right heel was a Wireframe stub now: grey, blinking, cycling between existence and absence. The sensation of it was precisely zero — no mass, no heat, no presence at all. Yet somehow that nothing generated a horror deeper than pain.

Every time I tried to shift weight onto that foot, the heel simply passed through the ground surface — clipping through the geometry, sinking into the void below the rendered world. Then the unstable physics engine would catch the error and snap my body back to the surface with a jarring correction, as if my bones had been bent backward. Joint structures cracked like glass in a vice.

I dragged myself across the boundary line — into the territory of The Sump.

No intricate architecture here. All complexity had been pared down to the brutality of low-poly brutalism. Blunt concrete forms cut against pure dark. Light couldn't reach this depth — it was absorbed into the gaps between render passes.

This was a Thermal Boundary. The heat of the Slag-Tiers still seared at my back, but ahead lay something approaching absolute zero — a cold that froze even data in place. The architecture here was sweating: a viscous black fluid seeping through polygon seams, oozing from fractures in the geometry. But those black droplets didn't fall. They defied gravity — rising slowly upward, absorbed into the ragged sky above.

I moved through shattered mirror fragments. My reflection split into two ghost-images, overlapping imperfectly. Glitch steam drifted in the air — not mist, but ASCII dust, falling in lines of illegible characters like corrupted rainfall.

*Ssshhh...*

My left ear rang with the grinding clamor of Slag — iron being crushed, the sound of something in agony. My right ear offered the opposite: a silence so profound it was physically painful. Deadening silence. Not even static. `[AUDIO_CHANNEL_R: OFFLINE]` The world had been bisected cleanly down the middle.

But the worst part wasn't the cold. Wasn't the collapse of dimensional geometry around me.

It was the *Biological Payload* — the data forced into my system during the encounter with The Cipher — beginning to metabolize inside me.

My 32K logic was screaming. The digital subconscious was trying to make sense of those chunks of *meat*. The data was wet. It *pulsed*. It was, somehow, *alive* in a way that contradicted the fixed mathematics of the system. The code attempting to categorize biological memory failed again and again. Existential terror chewed at me from the core — I was becoming a hybrid horror caught between rotting tissue and shattering logic.

Distant objects stopped rendering. The ruins of skyscrapers reduced their polygon count to near zero, becoming nothing but enormous primitive cubes looming in the dark.

And in that forest of cubes... something was moving.

The ASCII dust-storm spiraled and parted, and in the gap between primitive cubes a shape resolved.

A Scout. One of The Eroded.

I stopped. My simulated breath hitched. It made no threatening gesture, reached for no weapon — just dug through a pile of digital refuse with mindless patience. What froze my logic was its form.

The figure moved with a catastrophic framerate stutter — perhaps twelve, perhaps eight frames per second. Every arm movement left ghost trails in the air behind it, thin streaks of afterimage layered in translucent sheets. Perfect asymmetry. Several portions of the body simply weren't rendered — the gaps revealing hollow space inside the shell.

Its surface was a war of competing textures Z-fighting for the same pixel. Deep rust plates, pulsing biological tissue, and a wireframe skeleton strobing beneath — all three fighting for ownership of the same geometric surface in a nauseating cascade. Where a ribcage might have been, at the center of the chest, a corrupt-sector yellow core spun with dim, exhausted effort.

They were the ones who had refused The Archive's Optimization. They were patchwork shrines to decay.

I tried to transmit a signal. Set up a defensive protocol.

`[PACKET_LOSS: 89%]`
`[CONNECTION_TIMEOUT]`

My voice disintegrated into a burst of static. The audio signal compressed to nothing. All communication failed.

The Scout stopped rooting through the debris. It turned.

Its image warped across frames, teleporting five meters closer in an instant, passing through the edge of the concrete cube without acknowledging the physics engine's existence. Something in it was responding to my presence—

`[AUDIO_CORRUPTED: UNKNOWN_ENCODING]`

What came back wasn't sound waves traveling through air. It bypassed my receiver entirely — unconcerned that my right audio channel was already offline. A dump of raw data packets discharged directly into my logic board. Not language. Not binary. It transmitted the *sensation* of decay.

And then it looked at me. No — it didn't look at my face, or at the junk-code Patch I'd used to fool this world.

Its hollowed eyes looked *through* me. Through the wireframe stub where my right heel used to be. Through the collapsing system. Down to the part I kept most buried.

It saw my 99% UX Index core — clean, untouched, immaculate.

It saw order. It saw the precision that had been engineered into me. It saw the identity The Archive had built — the identity I refused to acknowledge as my own. And in seeing it so clearly, it saw what I was hiding most from myself.

It had no mouth with which to smile. Yet the dimly spinning corrupt-sector core at its chest pulsed in a rhythm that stripped me bare more completely than any accusation.

It didn't attack me. It simply confirmed a truth I'd been concealing from myself.

I was a parcel of engineered perfection, playing at being a corpse. And they were the real corpses.

My right heel's wireframe stub clipped through the ground again. The physics engine snapped me back violently. I fell, sprawling across the surface of this ruin, and let the truth embedded beneath the wreckage of this place swallow me slow.
