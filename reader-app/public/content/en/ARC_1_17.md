The Scout rotated away. Its blue scanner-light swept across the void above my head — missing me by millimeters. I held everything still, let it walk until the sound of servo motors dissolved into the silence. Then I wrenched my body out of the shadows and pressed forward, draining the last reserves of whatever I still had.

I put my weight down. The cracked data-surface splintered under me with the sound of compressing glass. My right heel was already gone — only the green-glowing Wireframe stub remained, blinking in the dark like a phantom limb that couldn't accept its own termination. Every time that wireframe stub connected with a surface, the Phantom Pain response triggered: a stutter in my 32K processing, a violent tremor in the physics of balance. I stumbled. The system tried to calculate the mass it could no longer locate, and the result was pain — a wave of it — snapping up through the digital nerve pathways as if the ghost of a heel were being bent at the wrong angle.

This was The Shatter-Points. Slum of decay. A dense, compressed community built across the surface of cracked obsidian mirror panels, the fractured interface zone between the Inner Ring above and the collapsed Mid-Sectors below.

The glass beneath my feet didn't reflect The Archive's perfection. It reflected what lay underneath the rendered world — biological laboratories drowned in pools of coagulated dark blood, hunks of pulsing flesh, rubber tubing coiled around server cables like veins extracted from a body and re-threaded through hardware. Seeping a viscous fluid like umbilical cord sap.

The light here was Data Phosphorescence — bruised purple, strobing like an infected wound. Glitch Spores drifted through the air. The taste of scorched ozone mixed with the copper reek of blood and rust.

Synesthesia attacked my neural system. The sound of tearing tissue and grinding bones played in syncopated rhythm with the high screech of a Dial-up modem. I could *see* the sounds — they sliced through the air as jagged red lines, cutting across my visual field, blurring the edges of my digital eyes.

Among the ruin, The Eroded emerged. Their avatars were assemblages of improvised horror — severed limbs replaced with raw blocks of unprocessed code, digital pus seeping from the junctions, their geometric surfaces reduced to coarse low-poly meshes with wet-looking gaps.

I pulled my Sepsis cloak tighter, trying to keep the anomaly hidden. But my system was being pushed past its ceiling. Automated Failsafe systems engaged — and the 99% UX Index core began radiating a High-Res Aura I couldn't suppress. Every step I took forced the decayed environment to re-render itself in pristine 32K — cracks sharpened, the rotting flesh of The Eroded around me rendered in exquisite horrifying detail. For them, that clarity was agony. They screamed. They scattered as if I'd ignited them.

At the same time — as I drew near the obsidian mirror shards — the Biological Payload embedded inside me began to quantum-resonate with the laboratory reflections below. It radiated Bit Rot outward in expanding waves. Code on every nearby surface rotted and collapsed in sequence, directly contradicting the clarity of my aura. I was a walking paradox — building perfection and spreading ruin in the same breath.

Then all sound cut out.

A figure stepped out of the shadow of a server umbilical-cable column.

Vesper, The Unpatched. Leader of The Eroded. His decay had reached Stage 3 — his avatar compressed into warped, fracturing geometry. The 32K aura emanating from me should have been tearing his form apart. The system error alarms were already shrieking. He stood anyway. He did not yield to my light.
