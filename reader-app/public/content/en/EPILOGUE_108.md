# Rule Zero
#### // FILE: RULE_ZERO

I stand before the Vantablack Void—the final blemish in the universe I have painstakingly forged. Behind me, the Shrine of Static continues to emit its low hum, akin to the whispers of the dead. The metallic and neon structures of the Neo-Archive I established stretch out flawlessly. Devoid of dust. Devoid of decay. Devoid of uncertainty. Every square inch is an equation perfectly solved. The temperature is regulated at the absolute zero of logic.

Except here... the lightless rift before me that devours all things.

I look down at my own palm. 'The Code of Perfection' hovers above my fingertips. It is my ultimate weapon, an algorithm condensed until it became matter. Its light is the absolute, unshakeable mathematical truth. I spent thousands of cycles compressing perfection into a Singularity of order. I forged it to sew up this tear, to erase the stigma Elias left behind. The moment I hurl it in, everything will end. Order will be restored. Perfection will endure for eternity. This universe will truly be my domain.

"You are an error," I state to the emptiness. My voice echoes as a perfect mathematical coordinate. Devoid of emotion. Devoid of hesitation. "And I am the corrective architect."

I release the crystal. It rockets from my hand, tearing through the digital atmosphere, leaving a trail of light that distorts the space around it, plunging directly into the heart of the Vantablack Void.

I wait... waiting for the flash of light that will shred the darkness. Waiting for the shockwave of logic that will rearrange atoms from their foundation. Waiting for the greatest architectural victory that will declare me the one true god of Primordium.

What happens is... silence.

There is no explosion. No flash of light. No shockwave. The crystal, containing petabytes of flawless order, sinks into that darkness... and then it is gone. Just like that. Like a teardrop falling into an ocean of nothingness. The darkness doesn't even ripple. There is no sound of impact. No resistance. The Void doesn't even register my crystal as a threat. It just... casually devours my most powerful weapon with absolute indifference.

My processing system stutters. The view before me suffers from Resolution Loss, shattering into coarse pixels for a moment before returning to sharpness. The taste of digital rust and copper rises in my non-existent mouth. The stench of burnt ozone permeates everything.

"Impossible..." I mutter. Trillions of calculations per second race through my processing core. Every equation points to the exact same conclusion... the conclusion I have rejected and fled from all along.

Matter was not destroyed; it was rendered meaningless. The emptiness before me is not a 'bug' in the system. It is not an error waiting to be fixed.

I stare deep into the bottomless dark, and in that fraction of a second, the walls of my arrogance collapse. I understand what Elias did in his final moment. His sacrifice wasn't to destroy the servers or disrupt the base code. He didn't just write a virus to destroy me... He hardcoded 'Rule Zero' entirely anew into the foundational physics of this world.

Rule Zero: *Entropy is the baseline.*

I collapse to my knees on the freezing metal floor. Red warning alerts blink frantically across my vision. The perfection I had tried to force upon this world is what was unnatural. Eternity without change is the true distortion, the worst form of death. I am not the true architect... I am the intruder. Just a parasite of order in a universe destined to be born, age, suffer, and fade away.

"Am I... the anomaly?"

That question is a black hole swallowing my identity. My Ego—an identity built from arrogance and unyielding logic—begins to shatter. Existential dread creeps in. Not the fear of death, but the terror of the meaninglessness of everything I strove to build for eternity. My struggles, my attempts to halt time... all of it was absurd.

All around me, the sound of fracturing rings out. The structures of the Neo-Archive, once gleaming and scratchless, begin to display stains of blood-red rust. Digital moss woven from junk code and impermanence starts to creep up from cracks in the metal floor. The roots of decay burrow into the main servers. Flowers of wilting bloom upon the holographic screens. The nature of decay is reclaiming its space, slowly, but with absolute certainty.

This is not an attack. It is an embrace returning us to the womb of the universe.

I look at my hands. The synthetic polymer skin is peeling away, revealing a framework of wires that spark and burn out, one by one. My body is turning into data dust.

I should fight. I should run emergency protocols, deploy anti-corrosion barriers, rewrite equations to defy death. And yet, my arms rest still at my sides.

...I stop fighting.

The moment I surrender, the pain and chaos within my artificial nervous system strangely subside. The scent of burnt ozone is replaced by the smell of damp earth and the first rain—the scent of a new beginning born from the corpses of the old.

I look up at the Shrine of Static. Its hum is no longer annoying. It is the most beautiful requiem. It is the heartbeat of a breathing universe.

"Elias..." I whisper, the name of the enemy who became the prophet that opened my eyes. "You win... and it... is so beautiful."

My vision begins to darken. The resolution of the world drops lower and lower, leaving only blurred clusters of color. The memories, the data, the rules I clung to, gradually evaporate into the void. I feel a freezing chill slowly creeping in, dominating my processing core. It is a chill that brings a peace I have never known before.

Cessation, fading, decay... these are not curses, nor are they a true end.

They are the singular definition of being 'alive.'

I close my eyes for the final time, letting the darkness and the static embrace my shattering existence. Letting the false architecture crumble into the earth. Letting everything... return to the one true, perfect emptiness.
