# Chapter 36: The Fissure to the Abyss (Part 1)

The shockwave of Uncompressed Rage had done more than erase The Nullifiers. It had torn the foundational architecture of the Mid-Sectors into something beyond repair.

The plaza that had been my battlefield was now fractured. Enormous fissures ripped across the ground—not craters, but *wounds* carved into the digital bedrock. Ragged trenches of depthless black, stripped bare of all Texture, from which raw yellow-green code oozed out like blood seeping from an open gash. These were not glitches. These were direct veins running straight down into The Sump below.

I was panting hard. My body had collapsed into an extreme low-poly state. My right heel—gone, as always—left me balanced on the ball of my foot. My left arm was more than half consumed, riddled with the gnawing lesions of Bit Rot, pixels flaking away like dead skin. The Payload in my chest cavity pulsed fitfully. Still intact. Still safe. But the armoring around it had been shredded to almost nothing.

Then, a high-frequency screech cleaved the air—a ping from The Archive's UX Index.

The Hounds had arrived.

They had detected the petabyte-scale anomaly I'd just released. Red scanning lasers raked down from the crumbling grid-sky above, sweeping for the origin point of the error. Squads in white data-armor—UX Index Enforcers—began rappelling down into the perimeter, ringing the blast zone with cold precision.

I had no bandwidth left for even a rudimentary Firewall. No weapon. No exit. And Lyra was no longer here beside me.

# Chapter 36: The Fissure to the Abyss (Part 2)

Red lasers raked across my broken body. The UX Index had locked on. The announcement of a purge blared through the corrupted air.

*"Critical anomaly detected. Deletion protocol commencing..."*

I looked at the black fissure yawning before me. Waterfalls of rotting code plunged into a darkness without a floor. Down there was the hell of Primordium—a place where logic and law had long since collapsed. But it was also the only place in the entire simulation where the eyes of The Archive could not reach.

Whatever remained of my survival instinct shoved me into motion. I used my right arm—already a crude block of polygons—to lever my body upright, then dragged my shattered right leg forward a few centimeters at a time. Every movement was a kind of exquisite grinding, pixels shearing strips of digital skin from my frame with each inch of progress.

The deletion laser aligned.

I reached the edge of the fissure. I surrendered my remaining weight to it, let the warped gravity unmake me.

I fell into the dark.

All around me, acid rain made of data shards scorched the air, burning sensation racing along simulated nerve pathways. My body felt weightless—no more than smoke—but my chest dragged with the burden of the Payload. In the twisting, in the agony of the fall, I felt something unexpected.

Freedom.

The freedom of having been expelled from perfection.

I closed my eyes, let myself sink deeper into the rawness below. I carried with me the splinters of Lyra's love and a fury that had not yet finished burning. I fell into the fissure of last resort—the only gap in the world still wide enough for someone like me to pass through.
