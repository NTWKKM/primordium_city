# Chapter 50: Sacrifice of the Discarded Logic - Part 1

We were cornered.

The signal sweeps of **The Hounds** beeped in rapid succession, like the heartbeats of a dying man. The path ahead was a dead end—a wall of void abandoned and unfinished by **The Archive**, a towering black barrier that swallowed everything.

I turned to look at Grit, the obsolete AI who was my sole comrade in this pit of code. His coarse polygonal body was covered in battle-worn fractures. He was calculating every available option, and the result of the equation was zero.

"Elias..." Grit’s voice was unnaturally calm. It was a silence free of static noise, unlike any tone I had heard from him before. "Do you know why I chose not to be deleted when my life cycle reached its end?"

I shook my head, my breath ragged and painful. The **Payload** still throbbed inside my chest. "We'll find a way. There has to be a crevice, something."

"I reject perfection. I believe that fractures are the proof of existence," Grit said, stepping forward, straightening his back to face the encroaching darkness. "And today, Elias, you are the greatest fracture **The Archive** has ever encountered... keep existing."

"Grit! What are you doing?!" I tried to lung forward to pull him back.

But my right heel betrayed me, and I crashed face-first onto the ground.

# Chapter 50: Sacrifice of the Discarded Logic - Part 2

"Initiating Overload Protocol... Force Self-Delete," Grit commanded his own processing core.

The fractures across Grit’s body flared with a brilliant, spectrum-piercing light. He was incinerating his entire code, releasing a century of accumulated static voltage and junk instructions in a single instant. As the pack of **The Hounds** lunged to erase him, the light detonated in a massive data bomb.

The shockwave from the self-destruction of this discarded logic swept out, blasting the black geometric storm of **The Hounds** apart. It tore open a **Data Void**, sucking them in and temporarily blockading the path.

"Goodbye... my anomaly," Grit’s voice broke apart, dissolving into pixel dust that scattered on the wind.

The blast was so violent it threw my body clean over the precipice behind me. I tumbled from the final cliff of **The Sump**, falling into the deep dark below... toward **The Abyss Edge**.

The final image I registered before my entire graphics system went black was the golden data dust of my old comrade drifting away. The agony of loss tore through my emotional subroutines, shattering them completely. I plummeted into the void, utterly alone, with the parasite still throbbing in my chest, and a left stump that felt nothing.
