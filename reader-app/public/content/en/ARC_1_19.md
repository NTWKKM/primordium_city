# Pulse from the Abyss

---

The Archive was destroying the void.

It was a Hard Render — the brutal forced-construction of an environment. The Dead Zone's mass was being overwritten with marble and concrete at full 32K resolution, a clarity so cutting it hurt to see. Spotlights, shadowless and white, detonated down from above, pulverizing the dark without mercy.

I was the target. My body blazed with a 32K aura, reflecting those beams like a lighthouse announcing its position in an informational desert.

*Step.*
*Wrench.*
*Frame Drop.*

The wireframe stub at my right heel slammed into the new concrete surface, sending a shudder of stutter cascading through the whole body. My balance fell apart. I moved with a broken, limping gait — animation logic corrupted, every step a lurching negotiation between what I intended and what the system could render in my degraded state. My right hand — the one I'd used to tear the tracking node out — was still a warped lump of misprocessed code, the wound at the base of my skull seeping digital gold that wouldn't clot. The Trauma Shard embedded in my left arm pulsed its aggravation into the rest of me. This wasn't blood. It was biological pain translated into digital collapse — my left arm fracturing into purple and green Artifacting, a stuttering rash of color-corruption along the seams that flashed in time with my pulse. The taste of rust and copper flooded my mouth, ozone burned in the back of the throat. Whenever the agony spiked, my rendering skipped — the system losing response for a fraction of a second, the world going silent and blank before snapping back.

They had arrived. The Nullifiers.

No face. No limbs. Only rings of chromium fractal geometry, stacked and spinning in perfect, endless concentric loops. They didn't run. They *relocated* — rewriting their own positional coordinates, appearing meters ahead of me in one frame, blinking to within reach in the next.

Their aura radiated outward — Throttling.

Everything slowed. Framerate crashed. The scene around me locked mid-motion like current turning to ice. I issued commands to my muscles: *Run. Run. Run.* The pain from the biological collapse screamed back: *Stop.* The system conflict savaged my processing core.

*Run.*
*Stumble.*
[System Conflict: Override Failed]
*Move.*
[Frame Drop: 12 FPS... 8 FPS...]

Green and purple Artifacting crept from my arm up to my shoulder. The chromium light of the Nullifiers strobed behind me. They couldn't lock a clean fix on my exact coordinates — the chaotic UX 99% emanating from the Trauma Shard pushed my positional data into constant flux. But they kept closing.

Ahead, a 32K marble wall rendered itself into existence — sealing off the exit. I was caged inside a prison of manufactured perfection.

The marble wall stood immaculate, seamless, flawless. The shadowless white light compressed me into a single blemish on an otherwise perfect world. The Nullifiers rewrote their coordinates in a ring around every vector of escape. Their fractal rings hummed at a frequency that made my synthetic nervous system feel like it was shaking itself apart.

If I maintained 32K status, they would lock the target and dissolve me into null.

I had to surrender the perfection.

My corrupted fingertips spasmed. I hauled the decayed arm up by will alone, forcing my subconscious to accept ruin. I issued the command to scale down my own processing — to embrace Resolution Loss. To cut myself loose from the anchor of high fidelity.

*Release.*
*Diminish.*
*Tear away.*

My rendering architecture cracked. Textures compressed to smears. The polygons comprising my body contracted, warped, and turned coarse. The sensation was being skinned — the pain of losing the high-resolution shielding hit without warning, my body becoming nothing but a confused mass of shivering, rotting data. Stuttering spread through every motion. Each step that pressed the wireframe stub down triggered Screen Tearing in the scene around me.

I forced my left arm — bent, warping from constant Artifacting — into a rigid arc and drove it at full force into the 32K marble wall.

*Crack.*
*Glitch.*

The arm didn't shatter. The Trauma Shard injected raw biological chaos directly into The Archive's architecture. Its algorithms attempted to compute, to render, to resolve *human pain*. They couldn't. The system's logic was built for perfection only. It had no equation for genuine torment.

The perfect wall suffered a Segmentation Fault. The high-end marble froze mid-render, then collapsed into a mosaic of green and purple blocks — the geometry's code unraveling into a Clipping Error, a void opening downward into undefined space beneath.

The Nullifiers blinked into close range, closing the net. They were too slow.

I didn't flee by hiding among garbage data — The Archive sweeps everything. Instead I chose the only exit the system hadn't accounted for: I stepped off the edge of the defined world.

I fell through the gap. Through the crumbling geometry. Out of bounds. Beyond the coordinate space The Archive could reach.

My low-poly, half-dissolved body plunged into an unfathomable dark. Above, chromium ring-light tried helplessly to overwrite the breach, sealing the hole behind me.

I was gone into the space the simulation had forgotten to make impossible.
