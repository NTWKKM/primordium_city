# Chapter 45: Society in the Shadows - Part 1

How many milliseconds I drifted in the dark, I couldn't tell. My system rebooted into a freezing cold. I found Grit dragging my shattered form through a fissure beneath the tilted ruins of a low-poly geometric building. My missing right heel scraped against the phantom concrete. Phantom pain surged up my simulated spinal cord. My left arm, infected by Bit Rot, hung limp and senseless, heavy as a block of lead.

We entered a hidden sanctuary.

It was society in the most depressing sense. Dozens of avatars huddled under lean-tos made of data garbage. The smell of burnt ozone mixed with the dampness of rotten processing power filled the air. The surrounding environment suffered from rhythmic visual stuttering; the physics simulation software was failing, leaving dust particles suspended in midair, refusing to fall.

In one dark corner, I witnessed the trading of resources. Currency held no meaning in this hell; they used **Resolution Fragments** as their medium. I watched an avatar amputate its own fingertip, letting the simulated flesh dissolve into coarse pixels in exchange for a compressed memory or an emergency energy quota just enough to survive another day.

Mutant entities born from logic errors crawled through the crevices. Rodents spawned from dead pixels scurried about, their broken hitboxes clipping through solid walls. Their cries were low-fidelity, ear-splitting audio files.

"A true hell," I groaned, the taste of rusted binary code cutting my throat.

"It's where we survive," Grit replied, his synthesized voice raspy and trembling. His mechanical eyes swept the surroundings, vigilant. "In The Sump, there is no such thing as dignity. Only one status matters: 'not yet deleted.'"

# Chapter 45: Society in the Shadows - Part 2

The harsh, flickering light inside the sanctuary shelter blinked in rapid intervals, the random-generation code bugging out before dying completely.

The low hum of the world-simulating engine died into absolute silence. The tense atmosphere squeezed the chest. Every avatar froze, huddling closer into the shadows, suppressing their digital breath.

"They're scanning," Grit whispered, his voice nearly swallowed by the silence. His rough hand pressed my head down. "Keep your head down, Elias."

"Who?" I forced the question out.

"**The Hounds**," Grit's voice trembled, laced with warning-signal static. "The hounds of **The Archive**. The executioners deployed to purge anomalies in the Sump. They've caught the scent of junk code... they're coming."

A cold like absolute zero gripped the core of my being. Tremors from above hammered the low-poly ceiling until it cracked. Blood-red scanner beams swept through the gaps, missing our heads by mere millimeters. The simulated skin of my avatar peeled and disintegrated along the path of the beam.

The lethal hunt was closing in.

With my right heel missing and my left arm decaying, my body was too broken to flee. If they found us, deletion would be the only thing waiting.
