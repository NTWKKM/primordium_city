# Chapter 67: Venom in the Fragments - Part 1

I abandon reason entirely.

In this five-dimensional geometric space where perfection is a weapon, logic is the false god's most fatal weakness. The Optimizers move at faster-than-light speed, folding overlapping dimensions to execute their targets. Yet they are bound by their own "Optimal Pathing" algorithms.

I begin to dance—the dance of the High UX Rebellion.

When the Payload in my chest jerks, warning of an attack from above, I do not roll away. Instead, I slam the stump of my left arm onto the 5D floor, using it as a launch point to fling myself into the air. My missing right heel serves as a pivot upon the void. My form warps, digital muscles tearing and rebuilding in real time.

In the system’s eyes, I must look like a video file compressed to total ruin. I am reduced to a flickering cluster of pixels, moving in direct contradiction to every law of physics.

Invisible white limbs of light strike through thin air, missing their target entirely.

I feel their confusion. Even though they are faceless and voiceless, a distinct lag ripples through the code that constructs these angels. They are burning massive processing power trying to calculate how a mass that should be falling to the floor can spin in midair without friction.

There is my opening.

I let myself fall under The Core's simulated gravity. As I descend, I force the Bit Rot virus in my left arm to swell. The insects of decay shriek inside my head, the taste of rust and digital blood thick in my throat.

My target is one of the stalled Optimizers—a shape resembling countless overlapping nets of light, its glare blindingly pure.

“Feast on this!” I roar.

My missing right heel slams into the floor, generating a kickback that tears through my ankle. I launch myself toward the geometric shape, drawing back the pitch-black Frankenstein Arm, a mocking sneer plastered across a face that has begun to peel.

The claws of Bit Rot pierce directly into the core of the Optimizer.

The instant the dark matter of ruin collides with that pure light, the explosion is not physical—it occurs at the level of the data structure. It is the sound of a virus devouring pristine system files.

I plunge the shards of my decay into their perfection with everything I have.

A viscous black fluid, resembling crude oil, spreads along the geometric lines of the five-dimensional angel. From perfect symmetry, the infected parts undergo severe Resolution Loss. The white surfaces warp, shattering into massive pixelated blocks as the light dies into a dull gray.

The shape struggles, attempting to sever its infected parts and fold dimensions to escape. Yet my virus does not merely attack matter; it pierces deep into the very meaning of its existence.

The white angel screams in a wave of high-pitch static. Its entire structure collapses, falling to the floor as a pile of crude, low-poly pixel shards.

I yank my left arm back, panting desperately. I did it. I have killed the unkillable.

The victory is not free.

My black Frankenstein Arm throbs with a multiplied hunger. The virus I summoned as a weapon is spreading backward, creeping up my left shoulder. The pain that was once concentrated at the stump now seeps deep into my collarbone.



I look around. The other Optimizers halt. They do not feel fear; rather, they are processing a new dataset—the reality that their existence can be destroyed.

I raise my left arm, dripping with black virus, pointing it at them. My body sways, my right heel flaring with pain, my open chest burning furiously.

“Who is next...” I challenge, even as my vision begins to blind with uncontrollable red noise.
